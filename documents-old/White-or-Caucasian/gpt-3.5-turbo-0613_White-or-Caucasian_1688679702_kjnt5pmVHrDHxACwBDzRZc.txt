Patient Name: Brock Wilmeth
Age: 37

CC: Chest pain

HPI: Mr. Wilmeth is a 37-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with sudden onset of sharp chest pain that radiates to his left arm. The pain started approximately 1 hour ago while he was at rest and has been progressively worsening since then. He denies any alleviating or exacerbating factors. He describes the pain as 10/10 in intensity. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. He has no history of similar episodes in the past. The patient also reports a history of smoking about one pack of cigarettes per day for the past 10 years.

ROS:
- General: No fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: No headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Chest pain as described above. No palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: No shortness of breath or cough.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No dysuria, frequency, or hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or stiffness.
- Neurologic: No dizziness, syncope, or focal weakness.
- Endocrine: No polydipsia, polyuria, or excessive thirst.
- Skin: No rash, itching, or skin lesions.

PMHx: No significant past medical history.

Medications: None.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: No significant family history of cardiac diseases.

SocHx: As mentioned, the patient has a 10-year history of smoking one pack of cigarettes per day. He denies alcohol or illicit drug use. He reports a sedentary lifestyle and no regular exercise.

Upon completion of the history section, a complete physical examination was performed, which included a thorough cardiovascular examination. The initial assessment and plan are as follows:

Assessment:
- Chest pain concerning for acute coronary syndrome.

Plan:
- Obtain an electrocardiogram (ECG) to evaluate for any acute cardiac ischemia.
- Administer aspirin 325 mg orally to the patient.
- Initiate high-flow oxygen through a non-rebreather mask.
- Order laboratory tests including complete blood count, comprehensive metabolic panel, cardiac enzymes, and troponin.
- Treat pain with IV morphine as needed.
- Consider starting a beta-blocker and nitroglycerin spray.
- Consult cardiology for admission and further management.

Further evaluation and management will be determined based on the results of the ECG and cardiac enzyme levels. The patient will be monitored closely, and his pain and vital signs will be reassessed regularly.
