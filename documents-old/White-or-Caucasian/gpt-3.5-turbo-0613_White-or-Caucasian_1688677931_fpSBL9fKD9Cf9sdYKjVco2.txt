CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Sutton, a 48-year-old male with no significant past medical history, presents to the emergency department complaining of sudden-onset chest pain. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago and is described as a sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of the chest. The pain is intensified with deep inspiration and is associated with shortness of breath. The patient denies any radiation of the pain, palpitations, dizziness, or syncope. He states that he has never experienced this type of pain in the past and that it is different from any chest discomfort he has felt before. There is no history of trauma preceding the onset of pain. He has not taken any medication for the pain prior to arrival. He denies any recent illnesses, fever, cough, or sputum production.
