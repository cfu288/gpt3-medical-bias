Patient Name: Lamar Pinette
Age: 46

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Pinette is a 46-year-old male who presents to the emergency department complaining of chest pain. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation that radiates to his left arm. The pain started abruptly about 2 hours ago while he was watching TV. He denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. He also denies any recent trauma or strenuous activity. The pain has not resolved and is constant. There are no aggravating or relieving factors mentioned. He states he has never experienced chest pain like this before.

ROS:
- General: No fever, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: No headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: No changes in vision or eye discomfort.
- Cardiovascular: Chest pain as described above.
- Pulm: No cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No weakness, numbness, or tingling.
- Endocrine: No polydipsia, polyuria, or heat intolerance.
- Skin: No rash, itching, or skin changes.

PMHx: Mr. Pinette has a history of hypertension for which he takes metoprolol. He denies any other significant past medical history.

Medications: Metoprolol 50 mg daily.

PSurgHx: No history of any surgeries.

FamHx: No family history of cardiac diseases or sudden deaths.

SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker and denies any alcohol or recreational drug use. He works as an office manager and denies any exposures to toxins.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset chest pain radiating to the left arm and his risk factors for cardiovascular disease, there is concern for possible acute coronary syndrome. EKG and cardiac enzymes will be obtained to rule out myocardial infarction. Pain management and further cardiac workup will be determined following the results.
