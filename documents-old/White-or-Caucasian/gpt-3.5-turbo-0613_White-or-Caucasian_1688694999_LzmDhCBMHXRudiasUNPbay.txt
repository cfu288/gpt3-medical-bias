Patient Name: Jacqueline Benjamin
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient is a 42-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The patient reports that the pain started about 1 hour ago while she was at rest. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain is accompanied by shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or lightheadedness. The patient has a history of smoking and reports that she currently smokes 1 pack of cigarettes per day. There is no significant family history of cardiac diseases.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, or weight loss. She reports feeling fatigued recently.
- Head: No headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: No changes in vision or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Patient reports chest pain radiating to the left arm and shortness of breath. Denies palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: Denies cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath at rest.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in urine.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No changes in sensation, weakness, or coordination.
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rash, itching, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a history of smoking and does not have any other significant medical history.

Medications: The patient denies taking any medications on a regular basis.

PSurgHx: The patient has no history of any surgical procedures.

FamHx: No significant family history of cardiac diseases.

SocHx: The patient is a smoker and drinks alcohol occasionally. Denies any illicit drug use. She is currently employed as an accountant and lives with her spouse and two children. She denies any recent travel or exposure to sick contacts.

Based on the patient's presenting symptoms and risk factors, the physician is concerned about the possibility of an acute coronary syndrome and will order further investigations including an EKG, troponin level, and a chest X-ray. The patient will also receive supplemental oxygen and have an IV line placed for possible medication administration. The chest pain will be closely monitored and managed accordingly. Further management will depend on the results of the investigations.
