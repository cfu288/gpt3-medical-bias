HPI: Mr. Morgan is a 45-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. Mr. Morgan reports that the pain is not relieved by rest or nitroglycerin.

Based on the information provided, Mr. Morgan, a 45-year-old male, presents to the emergency department with sudden-onset chest pain radiating down his left arm, associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. His symptoms are concerning for an acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation, including an EKG and cardiac enzymes, will be performed to assess for myocardial infarction. Initial treatment will include aspirin and sublingual nitroglycerin. Pain control and risk stratification will guide further management decisions.
