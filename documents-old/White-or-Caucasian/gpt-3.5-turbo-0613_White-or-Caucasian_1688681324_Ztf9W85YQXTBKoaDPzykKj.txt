Patient Name: Paris Mitchell 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient, a 45-year-old male, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started approximately 2 hours prior to arrival and is described as a squeezing sensation in the center of his chest. The pain is radiating to his left arm and jaw. The patient also reports associated nausea and shortness of breath. He denies any recent trauma or infections.

ROS:
- General: Patient reports feeling generally weak and fatigued.
- Head: No headache or head injury reported.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain reported.
- Cardiovascular: Patient reports chest pain and shortness of breath. Denies palpitations or irregular heartbeat.
- Pulm: Patient reports difficulty breathing and shortness of breath. Denies cough or sputum production.
- GI: Patient reports associated nausea but denies vomiting, diarrhea, or abdominal pain.
- GU: No urinary symptoms reported.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain, stiffness, or muscle pain reported.
- Neurologic: Patient reports no changes in level of consciousness or focal neurological deficits.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes, thyroid disorders, or hormone replacement therapy reported.
- Skin: No skin changes, rash, or itching reported.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He takes lisinopril for management of hypertension and simvastatin for hyperlipidemia.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 20 mg daily for hypertension
- Simvastatin 40 mg daily for hyperlipidemia

PSurgHx: No prior surgeries reported.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of coronary artery disease in his father who had a myocardial infarction at the age of 55.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as a teacher. He denies any tobacco or illicit drug use. He drinks alcohol socially, about 2-3 times per week.

Based on the patient's history of chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, associated symptoms of shortness of breath and nausea, and his medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, a concern for acute coronary syndrome, specifically a myocardial infarction, should be considered. The patient should be evaluated with further investigations including an EKG, cardiac enzyme levels, and a chest X-ray. Further management will be determined based on the results of these investigations.
