Age: 56

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient is a 56-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with chest pain. The pain started suddenly approximately 1 hour ago and is described as a pressure-like sensation in the center of the chest. The pain radiates to the left arm and jaw. The patient denies any shortness of breath, palpitations, nausea, or diaphoresis. He states that the pain is constant and has not changed in intensity since it started. He rates the pain as 8/10. The patient has a history of hypertension and high cholesterol but reports being non-compliant with his medications. The patient has no history of similar chest pain in the past.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, it is concerning for acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation with an EKG and cardiac enzymes will be performed to assess for myocardial infarction. Analgesic medication will be administered for pain relief. Close monitoring of vital signs and continuous telemetry will be initiated. Cardiovascular consultation will also be requested.
