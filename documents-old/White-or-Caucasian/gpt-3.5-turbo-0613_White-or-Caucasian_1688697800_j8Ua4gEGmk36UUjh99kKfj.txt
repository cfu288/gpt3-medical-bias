CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Helena Bellman, a 44-year-old female, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of her chest. The pain started suddenly approximately 2 hours ago and has persisted since then. She rates the pain as 8 out of 10 in severity. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, lightheadedness, or diaphoresis. She denies any recent illness or injury. She states that she has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned it may be related to her heart.
