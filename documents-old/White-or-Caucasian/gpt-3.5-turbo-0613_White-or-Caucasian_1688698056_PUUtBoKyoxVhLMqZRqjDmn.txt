CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a complaint of chest pain. She denies any recent trauma or injury. The pain is not relieved with rest or change in position. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, palpitations, nausea, or diaphoresis.

- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, lesions, or changes in the skin.

Medications: Lisinopril 20 mg daily, Atorvastatin 40 mg daily.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

Based on the patient's history of chest pain, radiating to the left arm and associated risk factors such as hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and family history of heart disease, there is concern for possible myocardial infarction or unstable angina.
