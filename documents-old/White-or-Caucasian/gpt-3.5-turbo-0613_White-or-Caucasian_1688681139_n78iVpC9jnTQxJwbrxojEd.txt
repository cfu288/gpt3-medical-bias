Patient Name: Tadeusz Jordan 
Age: 56

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Jordan is a 56-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The patient reports that the pain started suddenly approximately one hour ago while he was resting at home. He describes the pain as a sharp and crushing sensation located in the center of his chest, radiating to his left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath, diaphoresis, and nausea. The patient denies any recent trauma or illness preceding the onset of symptoms. He has no history of chest pain or myocardial infarction in the past.

ROS:
- General: The patient reports feeling weak and fatigued but denies fever or chills.
- Head: No headache or head injury reported.
- Eyes: No visual changes or pain reported.
- Cardiovascular: No history of palpitations or irregular heartbeats. No edema in the legs.
- Pulm: No cough, shortness of breath at rest, or wheezing reported.
- GI: Denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary symptoms reported.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling reported.
- Neurologic: Denies any focal weakness, numbness, tingling, or changes in sensation.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes or thyroid disease reported.
- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or skin changes reported.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He is also a former smoker, having quit 10 years ago.

Medications: The patient's current medications include lisinopril for hypertension and atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries reported.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension and ischemic heart disease in his father and siblings.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as a construction worker. He denies any recent travel or exposure to sick individuals. He admits to occasional alcohol use but denies any illicit drug use.

Based on the patient's history of sudden-onset chest pain, radiating to the left arm and associated with shortness of breath, diaphoresis, and nausea, a preliminary diagnosis of acute coronary syndrome, specifically an acute myocardial infarction, is suspected. Further evaluation is required to confirm this diagnosis.
