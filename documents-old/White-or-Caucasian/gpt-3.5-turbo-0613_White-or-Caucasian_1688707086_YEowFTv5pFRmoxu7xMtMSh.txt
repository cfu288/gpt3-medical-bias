Patient Name: Bert Trampe
Age: 62

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 62-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a complaint of chest pain. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. He states that he has never experienced this type of pain before and it started approximately 2 hours ago. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, nausea, or diaphoresis. The patient also reports that the pain is not relieved with rest. He states that the pain is constant and rates it as a 7 out of 10 in intensity.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension, dyslipidemia, and type 2 diabetes.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries reported.

The patient's chest pain, along with his risk factors for cardiovascular disease, raises concerns for acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation, including an EKG and cardiac biomarkers, will be obtained to determine the cause of his chest pain. Pain control measures will be implemented, and the patient will be monitored closely while awaiting results. The patient will also receive aspirin and sublingual nitroglycerin as per protocol. Cardiology consultation will be requested for further management.
