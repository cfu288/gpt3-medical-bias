Patient Name: Gary Hartsfield 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Hartsfield, is a 58-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. The pain started abruptly approximately 1 hour prior to arrival and is described as a pressure-like sensation in the center of the chest. The pain is radiating to his left arm and jaw. Mr. Hartsfield also reports associated shortness of breath and diaphoresis. He denies any nausea or vomiting. He states that he has never experienced this type of pain before and it is concerning for him.

ROS:

- Pulm: Denies cough, hemoptysis, or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.

- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: Mr. Hartsfield has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He is not currently following up with any physicians regularly.

Medications: Lisinopril 10mg daily for blood pressure management and Atorvastatin 40mg daily for cholesterol control.

PSurgHx: No prior surgeries.

FamHx: Father passed away at age 62 from a heart attack. Mother is alive and has hypertension and diabetes.

SocHx: Mr. Hartsfield currently works as an office manager. He is married and lives with his wife. He denies any tobacco or illicit drug use. He drinks alcohol socially on weekends.

Based on the patient's clinical presentation of chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, associated with diaphoresis and shortness of breath, and a past medical history significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia, the concern for cardiac etiology, particularly myocardial infarction, is high. Further investigation and management are warranted.
