Patient Name: Trudi Hohler 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 42-year-old female presenting to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a central, crushing sensation that started suddenly about an hour ago while she was at rest. The pain is radiating to her left shoulder and down her left arm. She denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. She states that she has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned it could be a heart attack. She states that the pain is not relieved by rest or nitroglycerin.

- Eyes: The patient denies any changes in vision, eye pain, or redness.
- Cardiovascular: In addition to the chest pain mentioned above, the patient denies any palpitations or other cardiac symptoms.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urinary habits.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain, swelling, or stiffness.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any headache, dizziness, or neurologic changes.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any polyuria, polydipsia, or excessive thirst.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes mellitus. She is currently on amlodipine and metformin for these conditions.

Medications: Amlodipine 5 mg daily, metformin 1000 mg twice daily.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's family history is significant for coronary artery disease in both of her parents.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She is married and works as an office manager. She lives a sedentary lifestyle and does not exercise regularly.

Given the patient's presentation of central, crushing chest pain radiating to the left shoulder and arm, concerning for possible myocardial infarction, further evaluation and management are indicated. The patient will be evaluated with an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and an urgent cardiology consultation will be obtained.
