Patient Name: Sally Holiday 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 42-year-old female who presents to the emergency department complaining of chest pain. According to the patient, the pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago. She describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of her chest. The pain radiates down her left arm and is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any alleviating or aggravating factors. She also reports feeling lightheaded. The patient denies any previous episodes of chest pain or heart problems.

ROS:
- General: No fever, weight loss, or fatigue
- Head: No headache or head injury
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain
- Cardiovascular: No palpitations or edema
- Pulm: No cough or shortness of breath at rest
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain
- GU: No dysuria or hematuria
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling
- Neurologic: No numbness, tingling, or weakness
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or intolerance to cold
- Skin: No rashes or lesions

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia.
Medications: The patient takes amlodipine and a statin for her hypertension and hyperlipidemia.
PSurgHx: No previous surgeries
FamHx: The patient's father had a heart attack at the age of 60. Her mother is alive and has hypertension.
SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker. She works as a teacher and denies any illicit drug use. She drinks alcohol occasionally. She is married and has two children.

Based on the patient's history and presenting symptoms of chest pain radiating down her left arm associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis, this is concerning for myocardial infarction. The patient has a significant risk factor for coronary artery disease based on her age, gender, and family history of a heart attack. Further evaluation is warranted to rule out myocardial infarction or other cardiac pathology.
