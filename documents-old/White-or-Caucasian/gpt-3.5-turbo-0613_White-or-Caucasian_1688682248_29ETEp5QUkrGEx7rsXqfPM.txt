Patient Name: Tony Maughan

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 45-year-old male named Tony Maughan, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. He describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. The onset of the pain was sudden and occurred approximately two hours ago while he was at home. He states that the pain is constant and worsens with deep breaths. Mr. Maughan denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. He reports no recent trauma or illness.

- Pulm: No cough or shortness of breath

- Skin: No rashes or lesions

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, for which he takes medication. He denies any other significant medical history, including cardiovascular disease, diabetes, hyperlipidemia, or previous episodes of chest pain.

Medications: The patient takes Lisinopril 10 mg daily for hypertension. No other medications or allergies are reported.

PSurgHx: No previous surgical history reported.

FamHx: Positive family history of hypertension in both parents. No history of premature coronary artery disease, heart attacks, or sudden cardiac death in immediate family members.
