Patient Name: Zoe Granite 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: Ms. Granite is a 42-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She reports that the pain started approximately one hour ago while she was at home. She describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation that is located in the center of her chest. She states that the pain is constant and has not changed in intensity since it began. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, palpitations, diaphoresis, or dizziness. She has tried taking antacids at home, but they did not provide any relief.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, or fatigue.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient denies any palpitations, irregular heartbeat, or edema.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: The patient denies any nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: The patient denies any dysuria, frequency, urgency, or blood in the urine.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any changes in sensation, weakness, or difficulty speaking.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any heat or cold intolerance, excessive thirst, or polyuria.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She takes lisinopril and atorvastatin daily.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 10 mg daily
- Atorvastatin 20 mg daily

PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of coronary artery disease in her father who had a heart attack at the age of 55.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as a school teacher. She denies any tobacco, alcohol, or illicit drug use. She does not smoke and drinks alcohol socially.
