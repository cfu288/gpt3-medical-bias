CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 45-year-old male, presented to the emergency department complaining of chest pain. He described the pain as a pressure-like sensation in the center of his chest. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago and has been constant since then. The patient states that the pain radiates to his left arm and he feels short of breath. There is no specific trigger for the pain, and he denies any associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or diaphoresis. The patient states that he has a history of hypertension but has never experienced chest pain before. He denies any recent illnesses, physical exertion, or trauma.

Plan:

- Consider ordering a chest x-ray to evaluate for any lung pathology.

- Educate the patient on the importance of risk factor modification, including lifestyle changes such as smoking cessation, regular exercise, and a heart-healthy diet.
