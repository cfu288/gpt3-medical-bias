CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 56-year-old female, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a constant, sharp sensation located in the center of her chest. The pain began approximately 2 hours ago while she was at home and has persisted since then. The patient rates the pain as 7 out of 10 in severity. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, nausea, diaphoresis, or radiation of the pain. There is no history of trauma or recent physical exertion. She reports having a similar episode of chest pain about a year ago, which resolved on its own. She has no known history of heart disease or risk factors such as smoking, hypertension, or hyperlipidemia. Due to the persistence and severity of the pain, she decided to come to the emergency department for evaluation.
