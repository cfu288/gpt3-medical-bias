Patient Name: Sylvie Dubose 
Age: 60

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 60-year-old female presenting to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She describes the pain as a dull, squeezing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago while she was resting at home and has been persistent since then. The patient denies any associated shortness of breath, palpitations, diaphoresis, nausea, vomiting, or lightheadedness. She states that she has never experienced this type of pain before.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, fatigue, or weight loss.
- Head: No headache or head injury reported.
- Eyes: No changes in vision or eye pain noted.
- Cardiovascular: Besides the chest pain, the patient denies any history of heart disease, hypertension, or hyperlipidemia. No history of palpitations or irregular heart rhythms.
- Pulm: No cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea. No changes in appetite or bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in frequency, urgency, or appearance.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or stiffness reported.
- Neurologic: No history of strokes, seizures, or neurological disorders. No changes in sensation or motor function.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes, thyroid disease, or adrenal dysfunction.
- Skin: No skin rashes, lesions, or changes observed.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, both diagnosed 5 years ago. She is currently on medications for these conditions, including lisinopril and atorvastatin.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 10 mg daily for hypertension.
- Atorvastatin 20 mg daily for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: No history of surgeries reported.

FamHx: The patient's mother had a history of hypertension and her father had a history of myocardial infarction. No family history of malignancies or genetic disorders.

SocHx: The patient is a retired nurse. She is a lifelong nonsmoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. No recent travel or exposure to sick contacts.

Based on the patient's history of chest pain, the absence of associated symptoms such as shortness of breath or diaphoresis, and her past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, a preliminary diagnosis of stable angina is being considered. Further evaluation is warranted to rule out any acute cardiac events.
