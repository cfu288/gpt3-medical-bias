Patient Name: Bryan Moldovan 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 45-year-old male, presents to the emergency department with complaints of sudden onset of severe chest pain that radiates to his left arm. He describes the pain as crushing and tight, with a duration of approximately 30 minutes. The pain started when he was resting at home and has been persistent since then. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, nausea, or diaphoresis. He does not report any recent trauma or injury. The patient has no prior history of similar symptoms. 

ROS:
- General: No generalized weakness or fatigue.
- Head: No headache or head trauma. No dizziness or syncope.
- Eyes: No visual disturbances or blurred vision.
- Cardiovascular: No palpitations or orthopnea. No history of hypertension or heart disease.
- Pulm: No cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath. 
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. No change in bowel habits.
- GU: No dysuria, frequency or urgency. No hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling. No recent trauma or injury.
- Neurologic: No numbness or weakness in the extremities. No changes in coordination or balance.
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or skin changes.

PMHx: The patient reports no significant past medical history. He denies any chronic medical conditions or previous hospitalizations.

Medications: The patient denies taking any medications on a regular basis. He does not report any allergies to medications.

PSurgHx: The patient has no history of prior surgeries or procedures.

FamHx: The patient reports no significant family history of cardiovascular disease or any other major illnesses.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as an accountant. He denies smoking, alcohol consumption, or illicit drug use. He does not engage in regular exercise but leads a fairly sedentary lifestyle.

Based on the patient's presentation of sudden onset of severe chest pain radiating to the left arm, concerning for a cardiac etiology, further evaluation including an electrocardiogram, cardiac enzymes and a chest radiograph will be performed to assess for acute coronary syndrome. Pain management and close monitoring will be initiated while waiting for further evaluation and consultation with the cardiology team.
