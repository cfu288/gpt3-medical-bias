Patient Name: Joette Hart 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient, Joette Hart, is a 42-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She reports that the pain began approximately three hours ago while she was watching television at home. She describes the pain as a sharp, squeezing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain is exacerbated by deep breathing and movement. She denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. She also denies any recent trauma or strenuous activity that may have precipitated the pain. She states she has never experienced chest pain like this before and is concerned it may be a heart attack.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, fatigue, or weight loss.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: The patient denies any changes in vision or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Aside from the chest pain, the patient denies any palpitations, racing heart, or irregular heartbeat.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, difficulty breathing, or wheezing.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urine color.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient complains of left arm pain radiating from her chest but denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any weakness, numbness, tingling, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any symptoms suggestive of diabetes or hormonal imbalances.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, itching, or changes in skin color or texture.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She is not currently taking any medications for these conditions.

Medications: The patient denies taking any medications regularly. She occasionally takes over-the-counter ibuprofen for pain relief.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's family history is significant for coronary artery disease in her father. Her mother is alive and well with no significant medical conditions.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol or use recreational drugs. She is married with two children and works as an administrative assistant.

Based on the patient's symptoms and past medical history, the possibility of cardiac origin for the chest pain is a concern. Further evaluation and diagnostic workup are warranted to rule out an acute coronary syndrome. The patient will be further evaluated, including an electrocardiogram and cardiac enzymes, and appropriate treatment will be initiated based on the findings.
