Patient Name: Wendee Clark 
Age: 47

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 47-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started approximately 2 hours prior to arrival and is described as a crushing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to both arms. The pain is accompanied by shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The patient states that she has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned that she may be having a heart attack. There is no history of trauma or recent illness. The patient denies any other associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or dizziness.

ROS:
- General: No fevers, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: No headache or changes in vision.
- Eyes: No eye pain or redness.
- Cardiovascular: Positive for chest pain, shortness of breath, and diaphoresis. Negative for palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: No cough or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No dysuria, hematuria, or changes in urinary frequency.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No headaches, syncope, or focal neurological deficits.
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes or skin lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. She has no known history of cardiac disease or diabetes mellitus.

Medications: The patient is currently taking lisinopril for blood pressure management and a statin for hyperlipidemia. She has also been prescribed a weight loss medication in the past, but states that she has not been taking it regularly.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a history of myocardial infarction at age 55. Her mother is alive and well with no significant medical history. She has one older brother who is healthy.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She works as an office administrator and is married with two children. She lives a sedentary lifestyle and admits to having a poor diet.

Based on the patient's history and symptoms of chest pain accompanied by shortness of breath and diaphoresis, the possibility of myocardial infarction must be considered. Further evaluation with an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and imaging will be necessary to rule out acute coronary syndrome.
