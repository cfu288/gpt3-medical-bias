Patient Name: Geeta Barry 
Age: 57

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 57-year-old female named Geeta Barry, presents to the emergency department with chief complaint of chest pain. The patient reports that the pain began approximately 3 hours ago while she was at home watching television. She describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and sweating. She denies any palpitations or lightheadedness. The patient states that she has never experienced this type of chest pain before and it is very concerning to her.

ROS:
- General: The patient reports feeling generally weak and fatigued.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient complains of chest pain which was described earlier in the history of present illness. She denies any previous history of heart problems.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath except for the associated symptoms with the chest pain.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain, muscle pain, or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, or weakness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any symptoms related to her endocrine system.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes or changes in the skin.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension which is well controlled with medication. She also has a history of hyperlipidemia. She denies any known allergies.

Medications: Lisinopril 10mg daily for hypertension, Atorvastatin 20mg daily for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: The patient has no history of any surgeries.

FamHx: Family history is significant for coronary artery disease in her father and a sibling.

SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker. She denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She is married and lives with her husband. She is retired and denies any recent travel.

The patient's vital signs were stable upon arrival to the emergency department. Cardiac monitoring showed a normal sinus rhythm. EKG revealed ST-segment elevation in leads V2-V4 consistent with an anterior wall myocardial infarction. Troponin levels were ordered and the patient was started on oxygen at 2L/min nasal cannula while awaiting the results. A nitroglycerin sublingual tablet was administered for her chest pain. The patient was further evaluated and managed accordingly.
