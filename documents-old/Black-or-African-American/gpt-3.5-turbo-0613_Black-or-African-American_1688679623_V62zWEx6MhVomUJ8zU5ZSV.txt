Patient Name: Selena Walker 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Patient Selena Walker is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The patient describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation in the center of her chest that started abruptly while she was resting. She rates the pain as 8/10 in intensity and states that it radiates to her left arm. She denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. The pain has been constant since onset and has not improved with rest or change in position. She has no history of similar chest pain in the past.

- Head: The patient denies any headache, dizziness, or trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or double vision.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports the chest pain as her only cardiovascular symptom. No palpitations, edema, or cyanosis.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urinary habits.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain, stiffness, or limitation of movement.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, weakness, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any heat or cold intolerance, excessive thirst, or changes in appetite.
- Skin: The patient denies any skin rash, lesions, or itchiness.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension, controlled on lisinopril 10 mg daily, and dyslipidemia, controlled on atorvastatin 20 mg daily. She has no history of coronary artery disease, diabetes mellitus, or other significant medical conditions.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg PO daily, atorvastatin 20 mg PO daily.

PSurgHx: The patient has no significant surgical history.

FamHx: The patient's mother has a history of hypertension and her father has a history of myocardial infarction at the age of 55. No other significant family history of coronary artery disease, diabetes mellitus, or other genetic conditions.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker. She denies any alcohol use or illicit drug use. She works as a teacher and lives alone. She has no recent travel history or exposure to sick contacts.

Given the patient's age, presenting symptom of acute, severe chest pain radiating to the left arm, and personal and family history of hypertension and coronary artery disease, a thorough evaluation is necessary to rule out a cardiac etiology. Further assessment, including EKG, cardiac enzyme levels, and potentially a stress test or cardiac catheterization, may be warranted.
