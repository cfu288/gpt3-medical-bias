Patient Name: Tanisha Gilyard 
Age: 38

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 38-year-old female presenting to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She states that the pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago while she was at work. She describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of her chest. The pain is radiating to her left arm and is associated with shortness of breath. The patient denies any nausea, vomiting, diaphoresis, or lightheadedness. She has no history of similar symptoms in the past. The patient has tried taking over-the-counter antacids without relief. 

ROS:

- Head: No headache or head injury.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye discomfort.
- Cardiovascular: As described in the HPI.
- Pulm: Shortness of breath associated with chest pain.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: No urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or limb weakness.
- Neurologic: No numbness, tingling, or seizures.
- Endocrine: No excessive thirst or urination.
- Skin: No rash or skin lesions.

PMHx: No significant past medical history. Patient is not currently on any medications.

Medications: None.

PSurgHx: No prior surgeries.

FamHx: No significant family history of cardiac diseases.

SocHx: The patient denies smoking, alcohol use, and illicit drug use. She works full-time as a teacher. She is married and lives with her spouse and two children. She has no recent travel history.

With these findings and the patient's symptoms, a further cardiac workup is indicated to rule out any acute coronary syndrome. A troponin level will be obtained, an EKG will be performed, and the patient will be monitored for any changes. A chest X-ray will also be ordered to evaluate for other potential causes of chest pain. Pain management will consist of nitroglycerin and analgesics as needed. The patient will be seen by the cardiology service for further evaluation and management.
