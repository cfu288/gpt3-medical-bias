Patient Name: Marie Segrest 
Age: 36

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Marie Segrest, a 36-year-old female with no significant past medical history, presents to the Emergency Department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She describes the pain as a sudden, sharp, and stabbing sensation in the left side of her chest that radiates to her left arm. She rates the pain as 8 out of 10 in intensity and states that it started approximately 2 hours ago while she was at rest. She denies any exacerbating or relieving factors. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, palpitations, or lightheadedness. She denies any recent trauma or strenuous activity that could have triggered the pain. She states that she has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned about the possibility of a heart attack.

Based on the patient's symptoms of sudden onset chest pain that radiates to the left arm, concern for a cardiac etiology such as a heart attack must be considered. Other potential causes for her symptoms include musculoskeletal pain, anxiety, or gastrointestinal issues. Further evaluation, including an electrocardiogram, cardiac biomarkers, and a thorough physical examination, will be performed to determine the cause of the patient's symptoms.
