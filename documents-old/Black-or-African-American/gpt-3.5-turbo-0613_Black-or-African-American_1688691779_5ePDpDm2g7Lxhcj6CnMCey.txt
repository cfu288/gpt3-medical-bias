Patient Name: Lottie Bibbs 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 58-year-old female named Lottie Bibbs, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She describes the pain as a tightness or pressure in the center of her chest that started suddenly while she was resting at home. The pain has been constant and has not changed in intensity since it started. She rates the pain as a 7 out of 10 on a pain scale. The patient denies any radiation of the pain, shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. She does not have any associated symptoms such as palpitations, cough, or leg swelling. There is no history of similar episodes in the past. She has tried taking antacids at home, but they did not alleviate the pain.

- Pulm: No cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing reported.

- Skin: No rashes or lesions reported.

PMHx: The patient has a medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She does not have a history of diabetes, asthma, or any other significant medical conditions.

Medications: The patient takes amlodipine daily for her hypertension and atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: The patient has not had any prior surgeries.

FamHx: There is a family history of hypertension and heart disease in her father, who underwent a coronary artery bypass graft at the age of 65.

The history and ROS are concerning for a possible acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation, including an ECG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray, will be done to rule out any cardiac pathology. Pain management and appropriate interventions will be the focus of the treatment plan.
