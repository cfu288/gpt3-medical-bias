Given the patient's chief complaint of chest pain, a thorough workup including an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest x-ray was initiated. The patient was placed on cardiac monitoring and given sublingual nitroglycerin for pain relief. The pain persisted despite nitroglycerin administration. A cardiology consultation was placed, and the patient was admitted to a monitored bed for further evaluation and management.
