Patient Name: Lacy Robinson
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She reports that the pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago and is located in the center of her chest. The pain is described as a squeezing sensation and is associated with shortness of breath. She denies any radiation of the pain, palpitations, or lightheadedness. The patient states that the pain is not relieved with over-the-counter medications and has been gradually worsening since onset. She denies any recent illness, trauma, or similar episodes in the past. No other associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or diaphoresis are reported.

ROS:
- General: No fever, chills, or night sweats; no weight loss or gain.
- Head: No headaches, dizziness, or syncope.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports chest pain and associated shortness of breath. No palpitations, orthopnea, or paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea. No history of heart disease.
- Pulm: The patient has shortness of breath associated with chest pain. No cough, wheezing, or hemoptysis.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea.
- GU: No dysuria, frequency, urgency, or hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain, swelling, or stiffness reported.
- Neurologic: No headaches, focal weakness, numbness, or tingling.
- Endocrine: No symptoms of hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism reported.
- Skin: No rashes, itchiness, or changes in moles reported.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, for which she takes lisinopril. She denies any other chronic medical conditions or significant illnesses.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg daily.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries reported.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of heart disease in both maternal and paternal grandparents. Her mother was diagnosed with hypertension and her father had a myocardial infarction in his 50s.

SocHx: The patient is married, works as a nurse, and denies any tobacco or alcohol use. She exercises regularly and follows a healthy diet.

Physical Exam:
- Vital signs: Blood pressure 130/80 mmHg, heart rate 85 beats per minute, respiratory rate 18 breaths per minute, temperature 98.6°F (36.9°C), oxygen saturation 98% on room air.

- Cardiovascular: Regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs, rubs, or gallops appreciated. No jugular venous distention or peripheral edema.
- Respiratory: Clear breath sounds bilaterally, no wheezing or crackles.
- Abdomen: Soft, non-tender, and non-distended. No hepatosplenomegaly or masses palpated.
- Extremities: No cyanosis, clubbing, or edema. Peripheral pulses intact.

A 12-lead EKG showed sinus rhythm with no ST-segment changes or T-wave inversions. Troponin levels were within normal limits. Chest X-ray revealed no acute cardiopulmonary abnormalities.

Based on the patient's symptoms, history, and physical examination, the differential diagnosis includes angina, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), and musculoskeletal chest pain. Further evaluation, including cardiac enzyme testing and cardiac stress testing, is warranted to rule out possible cardiac causes of her chest pain.
