Patient Name: Georgina Elliott 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department complaining of acute onset chest pain. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago and is described as a sharp, stabbing sensation. She rates the pain as 8 out of 10 in intensity. The pain is located in the central chest and radiates to her left arm. The patient reports associated symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any known triggers for the pain and states that it is different from any previous episodes of chest pain she has experienced. She also denies any recent trauma or injury.

PSurgHx: Patient denies any prior surgeries.
FamHx: Noncontributory.

Based on the patient's history of acute-onset chest pain, radiating to the left arm and associated symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis, a thorough evaluation is warranted to rule out a cardiac cause. Differential diagnoses to consider include acute coronary syndrome, myocardial infarction, pericarditis, or pulmonary embolism. Further evaluation will include an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and imaging studies as indicated. Pain control will be provided as needed.
