Patient Name: Henry Wade 
Age: 55 

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. He reports that the pain began suddenly approximately one hour ago while he was resting at home. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. He states that the pain has not improved or worsened since it started. He denies any previous episodes of chest pain or similar symptoms. He has no known cardiac history.

- Head: No headache or head injury.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Chest pain as described above.
- Pulm: No cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting.

- Neurologic: No weakness, numbness, or changes in coordination.

- Skin: No rashes, itching, or changes in moles or lesions.

PMHx: No significant past medical history. He denies any known cardiac conditions, diabetes, hypertension, or hyperlipidemia.

Medications: The patient denies taking any medications on a regular basis. He does not take any over-the-counter medications or supplements.

PSurgHx: The patient has no previous surgical history.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension in his mother and a family history of type 2 diabetes in his father. He denies any other significant family history of cardiac conditions or other chronic diseases.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as a construction worker. He denies any tobacco use and reports rarely drinking alcohol. He denies any illicit drug use. He has no known allergies.

Given the patient's age and presentation of chest pain radiating to the left arm, further evaluation is warranted to rule out an acute coronary syndrome. Possible differentials include myocardial infarction, angina, and other cardiac etiologies. The patient will undergo further evaluation and diagnostic tests to determine the cause of his chest pain.
