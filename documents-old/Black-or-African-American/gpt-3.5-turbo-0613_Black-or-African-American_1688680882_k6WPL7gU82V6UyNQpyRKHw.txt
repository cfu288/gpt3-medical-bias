Patient Name: Annie Jones
Age: 65

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient, Annie Jones, a 65-year-old female, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She states the pain started suddenly approximately two hours ago. The pain is described as a crushing sensation and is located in the center of her chest. The pain radiates to her left arm. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. The patient states that she has a history of hypertension and takes medication for it.

- Head: The patient denies any headache or changes in vision.
- Eyes: The patient denies any changes in vision or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports chest pain, but denies any palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough or shortness of breath.
- GI: The patient denies any nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports radiating pain to her left arm.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any weakness or numbness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any excessive thirst or changes in appetite.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes or skin lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension.

Medications: The patient takes medication for hypertension, but she is unable to recall the name of the medication.

PSurgHx: No significant surgical history.

FamHx: The patient denies any significant family history of heart disease or other significant medical conditions.

SocHx: The patient is retired. She denies any history of tobacco or alcohol use. She lives alone and is independent with activities of daily living.

Upon further questioning, the patient reveals that she has never experienced this type of chest pain before and is concerned about the possibility of having a heart attack. Further evaluation, including an electrocardiogram and other relevant investigations, will be performed to determine the cause of the chest pain and the appropriate management plan.
