Patient Name: Anissa Lane

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Ms. Anissa Lane, a 35-year-old female, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain began this morning and is described as a sharp, stabbing sensation in the center of her chest. The pain is constant, worsens with deep breathing, and is not relieved by rest. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, palpitations, diaphoresis, or radiation of the pain. She denies any recent illness, trauma, or strenuous physical activity. The patient states that she has never experienced chest pain like this before and is concerned about her current symptoms.

The patient's chest pain is concerning for a cardiac etiology, given its location and characteristics. Further evaluation will include a focused physical examination, EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest x-ray.
