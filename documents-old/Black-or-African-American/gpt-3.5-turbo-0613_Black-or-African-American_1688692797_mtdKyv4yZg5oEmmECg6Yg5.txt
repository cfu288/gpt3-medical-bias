Patient Name: Darwin Jenkins 
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Jenkins is a 52-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. He reports that the pain started approximately 2 hours ago and is described as a pressure-like sensation. The pain is located in the center of his chest and radiates to his left arm. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. Mr. Jenkins states that he has a history of hypertension and high cholesterol, but has not been compliant with his medications. He denies any recent illnesses or injuries. The pain did not improve with over-the-counter antacids. The patient was concerned about the possibility of a heart attack and decided to come to the emergency department.

Further evaluation and diagnostic workup are warranted to rule out acute coronary syndrome.
