CC: Chest Pain

- Aspirin 81 mg daily

Given the patient's presentation with acute chest pain, further evaluation and workup are warranted to determine the cause. The initial differential diagnosis includes acute coronary syndrome, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), musculoskeletal pain, or other less common causes. The patient will be further evaluated and monitored closely, including an electrocardiogram (ECG) and cardiac biomarkers to rule out myocardial infarction. Pain management and appropriate referrals will be initiated accordingly.
