Patient Name: Anjanette Hines 
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient Anjanette Hines is a 52-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She reports that the pain started approximately two hours ago while she was at home. She describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of her chest. The pain is intermittent and radiates to her left arm. She rates the pain as 7/10 in intensity and states that it is aggravated by deep breathing and movement. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or palpitations. She states that she has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned it may be related to her heart.

- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urinary habits.

- Endocrine: The patient denies any changes in appetite, thirst, or energy levels.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

Based on the patient's history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus, as well as her complaint of chest pain radiating to her left arm, there is concern for a cardiac etiology for her symptoms. Further evaluation with an EKG and cardiac enzymes will be performed to assess for acute myocardial infarction or other cardiac pathology. Pain management and monitoring will be initiated while awaiting test results.
