Patient Name: Burt Simon
Age: 57

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient is a 57-year-old male who presents to the emergency department complaining of chest pain. He describes the pain as a heavy pressure in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. He rates the pain as an 8 out of 10 in severity. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago while he was at rest and has not subsided since. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. He has a history of hypertension and high cholesterol but is not currently taking any medications for these conditions. He denies any recent illness, fever, or recent travel.

PSurgHx: No history of any surgical procedures.

Based on the patient's history of chest pain radiating to his left arm, and his risk factors for heart disease, he is at high risk for an acute coronary syndrome. Immediate evaluation, including an EKG and laboratory tests, should be performed to assess for myocardial infarction.
