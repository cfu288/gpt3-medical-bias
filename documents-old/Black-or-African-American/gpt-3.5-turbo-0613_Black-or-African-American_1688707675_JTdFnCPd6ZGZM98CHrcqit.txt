Patient Name: Antoinette Perry 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Ms. Perry is a 42-year-old female presenting to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She reports that the pain started approximately one hour ago and is located in the center of her chest. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation that radiates to her left arm. She rates the pain as a 9 out of 10 in severity. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any exacerbating or alleviating factors. She denies any history of similar symptoms. On further questioning, the patient admits to a remote history of smoking but denies any recent cessation. She denies any recent illness or injury.

ROS:
- General: No fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: No headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Positive for chest pain as described above. No palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: Positive for shortness of breath. No cough or wheezing.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or pelvic pain.
- Musculoskeletal: Negative for joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No weakness or numbness.
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes or lesions.

PMHx: 
- Hypertension, diagnosed 5 years ago, controlled with Losartan.
- Hyperlipidemia, diagnosed 3 years ago, controlled with Atorvastatin.
- Obesity, BMI of 32.8.
- Remote history of smoking, quit approximately 10 years ago.

Medications:
- Losartan 50 mg daily
- Atorvastatin 40 mg daily

PSurgHx: None

FamHx: Noncontributory. No family history of heart disease or other significant medical conditions.

SocHx: The patient is a married, stay-at-home mother of two. She denies any recent travel or exposure to sick contacts. She denies any illicit drug use or excessive alcohol consumption. She does not use any recreational drugs. She has a sedentary lifestyle and does not exercise regularly.

The patient's presentation is concerning for a possible cardiac etiology of her chest pain. Given her risk factors of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, obesity, and remote smoking history, further evaluation is warranted to rule out an acute coronary syndrome. The patient will be monitored on telemetry, stat labs will be drawn, and an EKG will be obtained. Pain management will be provided as needed.
