Patient Name: Bart Newton 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Newton, is a 45-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a complaint of chest pain. The pain began approximately two hours ago while he was at work. The pain is described as a pressing sensation in the center of his chest, radiating to his left arm. He rates the intensity of the pain as 8 out of 10. The pain is accompanied by shortness of breath and diaphoresis. Mr. Newton denies any associated symptoms such as nausea or vomiting. There is no alleviating or exacerbating factor identified. He denies any similar episodes in the past.

Close monitoring of the patient's vital signs and pain level will be maintained.
