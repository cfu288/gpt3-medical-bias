Age: 62

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 62-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain began suddenly approximately one hour ago and has been constant since then. she also reports feeling short of breath and nauseous. There is no known precipitating event. The patient denies any previous episodes of similar pain. She tried taking over-the-counter antacids, but they did not alleviate her symptoms.

- Head: The patient denies any headache or visual disturbances.

- Pulm: The patient reports shortness of breath associated with the chest pain. No cough or phlegm production.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.

- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain, swelling, or stiffness reported.

- Endocrine: No recent changes in weight, appetite, or thirst reported.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, itching, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension for which she takes lisinopril. She does not know her current blood pressure or when it was last checked.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg daily.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries reported.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension in both her parents. Her mother had a heart attack at the age of 70.

SocHx: The patient is a retired school teacher. She denies any tobacco or alcohol use. She lives alone and does not have any pets.

Initial Assessment: 62-year-old female with sudden onset, sharp, stabbing chest pain radiating to the left arm associated with shortness of breath and nausea. Concerning for acute coronary syndrome.

Plan: Obtain an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest x-ray. Administer aspirin, nitroglycerin, and oxygen as needed. Discuss with the cardiologist for further evaluation and management.
