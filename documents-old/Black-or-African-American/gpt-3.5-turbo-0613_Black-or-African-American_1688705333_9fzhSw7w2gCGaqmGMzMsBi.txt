Patient Name: Norris Davis 
Age: 57

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 57-year-old male named Norris Davis, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. According to the patient, the pain began suddenly approximately 2 hours ago. He describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation located in the center of his chest. The pain is radiating to his left arm and jaw. He denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or lightheadedness. The patient states that he took an antacid at home but did not provide relief.

- Head: The patient denies any headaches or head trauma.

- Pulm: The patient denies any shortness of breath, cough, or wheezing.
- GI: No complaints of nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or changes in bowel movements.

- Endocrine: No complaints of excessive thirst, increased urination, or changes in appetite.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, lesions, or changes to his skin.

PMHx: The patient has a medical history significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia.
Medications: The patient takes lisinopril for hypertension and rosuvastatin for hyperlipidemia.
PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgeries.
FamHx: The patient reports a family history of cardiovascular disease, with both parents having a history of myocardial infarction in their 60s.
SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. He works as an office manager and lives with his spouse.

Based on the patient's presenting symptoms of sudden-onset chest pain with radiation to the left arm and jaw, concern for cardiac etiology such as acute coronary syndrome or myocardial infarction is high. The patient will be further evaluated and appropriate diagnostic tests, including an electrocardiogram and cardiac enzymes, will be ordered to confirm the diagnosis and guide management.
