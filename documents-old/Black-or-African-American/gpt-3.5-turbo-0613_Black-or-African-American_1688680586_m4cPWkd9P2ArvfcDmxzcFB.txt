Patient Name: Charlotte Dean 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. The pain started suddenly about two hours ago while she was at rest. She describes the pain as a crushing sensation in her chest that radiates to her left arm. She denies any shortness of breath, dizziness, or palpitations. She has no significant past medical history and denies any recent illnesses or injuries. The patient states that she has never had chest pain like this before and is concerned that it could be a heart attack.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fevers, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: No headaches or head injuries.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient complains of the chest pain as described above. No known history of heart disease, hypertension, or high cholesterol. No swelling or redness in the lower extremities.
- Pulm: No shortness of breath or cough.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in urine output.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain, stiffness, or swelling.
- Neurologic: No numbness, tingling, weakness, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: No known diabetes, thyroid dysfunction, or adrenal disorders.
- Skin: No rashes, itching, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: No significant past medical history. Patient denies any chronic illnesses or hospitalizations.
Medications: The patient is not currently taking any medications.
PSurgHx: No previous surgeries or procedures.
FamHx: The patient has a family history of heart disease, with her father and two siblings having had heart attacks in their 50s.
SocHx: The patient is married and works as a teacher. She denies tobacco, alcohol, or illicit drug use. She does not exercise regularly and admits to a diet high in processed foods.

Given the patient's age, symptoms, and family history, it is important to consider the possibility of a cardiac etiology for her chest pain. Further evaluation with an EKG and cardiac enzymes is warranted to rule out acute coronary syndrome.
