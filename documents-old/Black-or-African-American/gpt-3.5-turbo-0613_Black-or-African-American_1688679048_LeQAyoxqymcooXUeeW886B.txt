CC: Chest Pain

She reports that the chest pain started abruptly approximately 2 hours ago while she was at home rest. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of her chest, radiating to her left arm. She also reports associated shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any recent illness, trauma, or exertion prior to the onset of symptoms.

- Pulm: She denies any cough, hemoptysis, or wheezing.
- GI: She denies any nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: She denies any dysuria, frequency, or urgency.
- Musculoskeletal: She reports the radiating pain to her left arm.
- Neurologic: She denies any focal weakness or numbness.
- Endocrine: She denies any heat or cold intolerance.

Assessment:
1. Chest pain: Concerning for acute coronary syndrome versus angina pectoris.
   - Differential diagnosis includes myocardial infarction, unstable angina, pericarditis, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), musculoskeletal chest pain. 
   - Further diagnostic workup indicated including troponin levels, electrocardiogram, and echocardiogram.

Plan:
- Administer aspirin 325mg orally.
- Administer sublingual nitroglycerin for acute chest pain relief.
- Place the patient on cardiac monitor.
- Perform serial troponin levels every 3-6 hours.
- Obtain a stat chest x-ray.
- Consult cardiology for further management.
- Consider stress test and coronary angiography depending on cardiology evaluation.

Patient and family education provided regarding the importance of further evaluation for chest pain, signs of myocardial infarction, and the need for immediate medical attention. The patient was admitted to the hospital for further evaluation and management.
