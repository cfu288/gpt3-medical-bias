Patient Name: Freddie Smith 
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Smith is a 52-year-old male who presents to the emergency department complaining of chest pain. The pain started suddenly while he was at rest about 2 hours ago. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm and jaw. He rates the pain as an 8/10 in intensity. He denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. He reports no previous episodes of chest pain or similar symptoms. He states that his pain has not changed since it started.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: No headache or head trauma reported.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports no palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: No cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: Denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or stiffness.
- Neurologic: No numbness, tingling, or weakness.
- Endocrine: No excessive thirst, excessive hunger, or excessive urination.
- Skin: No rashes or changes in the skin.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, dyslipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He is currently on medication for these conditions, including lisinopril, simvastatin, and metformin. He reports good compliance with his medications.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 20 mg daily for hypertension.
- Simvastatin 40 mg daily for dyslipidemia.
- Metformin 1000 mg twice daily for type 2 diabetes mellitus.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries reported.

FamHx: The patient's father had a history of myocardial infarction at the age of 60. His mother is alive and has hypertension.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as a computer programmer. He denies tobacco, alcohol, or illicit drug use. He exercises regularly and follows a healthy diet.

Given the patient's sudden onset of chest pain at rest, radiating to the left arm and jaw, and associated with a crushing sensation, a thorough evaluation is warranted to rule out an acute coronary syndrome.
