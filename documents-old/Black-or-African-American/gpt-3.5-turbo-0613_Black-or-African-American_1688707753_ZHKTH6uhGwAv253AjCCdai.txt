Patient Name: Walter Gregory

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Walter Gregory, a 52-year-old male, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. He reports that the pain started suddenly about an hour ago while he was at home. He describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. The pain is rated as 7 out of 10 in intensity and is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. He denies any nausea or vomiting. Mr. Gregory states that he does not have a history of similar episodes in the past.

He was diagnosed with type 2 diabetes 10 years ago and has been controlling it with oral hypoglycemic medication. He had a myocardial infarction 5 years ago and underwent coronary artery bypass graft surgery.

Based on the patient's history of chest pain, radiation to the left arm, associated diaphoresis, and his past medical history of myocardial infarction, there is concern for cardiac etiology of the chest pain. Additional investigations including EKG, cardiac enzymes, and cardiac imaging will be ordered to evaluate for acute coronary syndrome. Pain management and supportive care will be provided in the meantime.
