Patient Name: Bettye Teal 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Bettye Teal, is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She reports that the pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago while she was at home watching TV. She describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm and jaw. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and sweating. She denies any nausea or vomiting. The patient states that this is the first time she has experienced this type of chest pain.

- Head: The patient denies any headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports chest pain and shortness of breath. She denies any palpitations or leg swelling.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath at rest.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary frequency, urgency, or pain.

- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, or weakness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any heat or cold intolerance, excessive thirst, or frequent urination.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, itching, or changes in moles.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She is not currently taking any medications for these conditions.

Medications: The patient does not take any routine medications.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of heart disease, with her father having a heart attack in his 50s.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, associated with shortness of breath and sweating, and her family history of heart disease, I am concerned for acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation and management are indicated.
