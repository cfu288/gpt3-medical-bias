Patient Name: Adelaide Wheeler 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 58-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The patient reports that the chest pain has a sudden onset and is located in the center of her chest. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation that radiates to her left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The patient denies any other symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or dizziness. She states that the pain started approximately 45 minutes ago and has been constant since then. There were no precipitating factors or alleviating factors. The patient has no prior history of chest pain or cardiac disease. She denies any recent travels, surgery, or trauma. There is no history of similar symptoms in the past. The patient has not taken any medications for the pain.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: No headache or head injury.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: + Chest pain as described above. No palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: No cough or shortness of breath at rest.
- GI: No abdominal pain or nausea.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No focal weakness, numbness, or changes in sensation.
- Endocrine: No changes in appetite or thirst. No heat or cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes or skin changes.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes mellitus. She has been noncompliant with her medications and has not followed up with her primary care physician for the past 6 months.

Medications: None reported.

PSurgHx: No prior surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a history of myocardial infarction at the age of 65. Her mother is alive with no significant medical history.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She works as a teacher and lives with her husband. She has no known allergies.

Based on the patient's history and presentation of sudden-onset chest pain radiating to the left arm, shortness of breath, and diaphoresis, a working diagnosis of acute coronary syndrome is suspected. The patient will be further evaluated with an electrocardiogram, cardiac enzymes, and a chest x-ray to assess for any acute ischemic changes or other potential causes of her symptoms. The patient will also be placed on continuous cardiac monitoring and given aspirin and nitroglycerin as needed for pain. A consultation with cardiology will be requested for further management and possible intervention.
