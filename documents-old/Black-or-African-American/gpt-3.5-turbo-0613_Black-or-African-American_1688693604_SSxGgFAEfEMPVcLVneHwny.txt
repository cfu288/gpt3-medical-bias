Patient Name: Gerard Murphy
Age: 54

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 54-year-old male named Gerard Murphy, presents to the emergency department with complaints of severe chest pain. The pain began abruptly approximately 2 hours ago while he was resting at home. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of his chest, radiating to his left arm and jaw. The pain is associated with shortness of breath, diaphoresis, and nausea. The patient denies any previous episodes of similar chest pain. He states that he has never experienced this level of pain before and is concerned that he might be having a heart attack.

- Head: The patient denies any headache or head injury.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or double vision.
- Cardiovascular: As mentioned earlier, the patient is experiencing severe chest pain radiating to his left arm and jaw. He also reports shortness of breath and diaphoresis. He denies any palpitations or irregular heartbeat.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, wheezing, or hemoptysis.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms.

- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness or weakness in his extremities, dizziness, or loss of consciousness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any symptoms suggestive of hormonal imbalances.
- Skin: The patient denies any rash, itching, or changes in skin color or texture.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and smoking. He is currently taking medications for his hypertension and hyperlipidemia; however, he is unsure of the names and dosage of these medications. The patient also reports a 20-pack year smoking history.

Medications: The patient is unsure of the names and dosage of his current medications.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of coronary artery disease in his father who had a myocardial infarction at the age of 60. There is no known history of other genetic or hereditary conditions in the family.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as an office manager. He denies any history of alcohol or recreational drug use. He reports smoking a pack of cigarettes per day for the past 20 years. He denies any recent travel, sick contacts, or exposure to infectious diseases.

Given the patient's history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, severe chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, associated symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis, and family history of coronary artery disease, a workup for acute coronary syndrome is appropriate. The patient should be evaluated urgently for possible myocardial infarction and further management should be determined based on the results of further investigations.
