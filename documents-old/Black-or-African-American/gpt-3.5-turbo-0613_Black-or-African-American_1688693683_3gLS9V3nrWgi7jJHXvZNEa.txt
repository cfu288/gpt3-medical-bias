Patient Name: Shanda Harris 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Shanda Harris, is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started abruptly approximately 3 hours ago while she was at home resting. She describes the pain as a sharp, squeezing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. She rates the pain as a 9/10 in intensity. She denies any exacerbating or relieving factors. The pain is associated with shortness of breath, diaphoresis, and nausea. She has no history of similar symptoms in the past. She denies any recent illness, trauma, or strenuous activities. She denies any recent travel or exposure to sick contacts. She has not taken any medications for the pain.

- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. She does not have a history of diabetes, asthma, or heart disease. She denies any previous surgeries.

Medications: The patient takes metoprolol for hypertension and a statin for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension and heart disease. Her father had a heart attack at the age of 60.

Based on the patient's presentation and risk factors, there is concern for a cardiac etiology of her chest pain. Further evaluation and diagnostic workup are recommended to rule out acute myocardial infarction or other serious cardiac conditions.
