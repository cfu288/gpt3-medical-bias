Patient Name: Leon Gates 
Age: 54

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Gates, is a 54-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago while he was resting at home. He describes the pain as a pressing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. The pain is accompanied by shortness of breath and diaphoresis. He denies any precipitating factors or relieving factors. He has no history of similar symptoms in the past and denies any recent illness or trauma.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: No headache, dizziness, or trauma.
- Eyes: No visual changes, double vision, or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: As mentioned earlier, the patient complains of chest pain that radiates to his left arm. No palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: The patient experiences shortness of breath. No cough or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in urinary habits.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports no joint pain or stiffness.
- Neurologic: No history of numbness, weakness, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: No symptoms of excessive thirst or urination.
- Skin: No rashes or lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension for which he takes metoprolol. He denies any other significant medical conditions, including diabetes, hyperlipidemia, or previous cardiac events.

Medications: Metoprolol 50mg daily.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father passed away at the age of 60 due to a heart attack. No other significant family history of cardiovascular disease.

SocHx: The patient is a former smoker but has quit approximately 5 years ago. He denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. He works as an accountant and denies any significant stressors in his personal life.

Given the patient's age, symptoms of chest pain radiating to the left arm, and associated shortness of breath and diaphoresis, there is a concern for possible acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation and management will include a physical examination, ECG, cardiac enzymes, and consideration for early revascularization if indicated.
