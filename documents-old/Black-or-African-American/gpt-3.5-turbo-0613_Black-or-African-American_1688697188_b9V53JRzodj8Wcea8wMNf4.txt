CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Patient is a 58-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of gradual onset chest pain for the past 2 hours. PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: There is no significant family history of heart disease or other medical conditions.

Given the patient's symptoms of substernal chest pain radiating to the left arm, associated with shortness of breath, palpitations, and diaphoresis, there is concern for acute coronary syndrome. A thorough investigation is warranted to rule out a myocardial infarction or other cardiac etiologies.
