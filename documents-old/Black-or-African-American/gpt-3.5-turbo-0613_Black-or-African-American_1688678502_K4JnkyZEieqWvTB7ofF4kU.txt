The patient's presenting complaint of severe, sudden onset chest pain requires urgent evaluation to assess for any cardiac etiology. Differential diagnoses for this patient may include acute coronary syndrome, pulmonary embolism, pneumothorax, esophageal rupture, or musculoskeletal chest pain. Possible diagnostic tests include a chest X-ray, cardiac biomarkers, and CT angiography if indicated. Pain control and reassurance will be provided to the patient while awaiting further evaluation.
