Patient Name: Beatrice Brown 
Age: 54

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 54-year-old female presenting to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She has tried taking over-the-counter antacids, but they did not provide significant relief.

Assessment:
1. Chest pain: Patient with acute onset chest pain, concerning for possible cardiac etiology.
2. Hypertension: Known history of hypertension.
3. Hyperlipidemia: Known history of hyperlipidemia.

Plan:
1. Administer sublingual nitroglycerin for chest pain.
2. Administer aspirin 325 mg PO for possible acute coronary syndrome.
3. Serial troponin levels and EKG monitoring.
4. Obtain a chest X-ray to evaluate for other causes of chest pain.
