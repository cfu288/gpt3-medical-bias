Patient Name: Raja Williams 
Age: 48

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 48-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest that started about 30 minutes ago. The pain is radiating to his left arm and jaw, and he rates it as 8 out of 10 in severity. The patient denies any alleviating or exacerbating factors. He reports associated symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The patient states that he has never experienced this type of pain before.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset crushing chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, along with associated symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis, there is concern for a cardiac etiology. The patient will be further evaluated with an EKG and cardiac enzymes. Pain management and appropriate monitoring measures will be implemented while waiting for the results. A cardiology consult will be obtained.
