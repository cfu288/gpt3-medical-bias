Patient Name: Ruth Richardson 
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Ruth Richardson, is a 55-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago while she was resting at home. She describes the pain as a tightness and pressure in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any nausea or vomiting. She reports no recent trauma or injuries. She states that she has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes. She denies any recent illnesses or infections.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: The patient reports no headache or head injury.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient complains of chest pain as mentioned in the HPI. She denies any palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: The patient has shortness of breath associated with the chest pain.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary frequency, urgency, or changes in urine color.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports radiating left arm pain associated with the chest pain.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any dizziness, syncope, or changes in sensation.
- Endocrine: The patient has a past medical history of type 2 diabetes, which is well controlled with metformin.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, itching, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: 
- Hypertension
- Hyperlipidemia
- Type 2 diabetes

Medications: 
- Metformin for type 2 diabetes
- Lisinopril for hypertension
- Atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's family history is significant for coronary artery disease on her maternal side.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as a teacher. She denies any tobacco, alcohol, or illicit drug use. She has a sedentary lifestyle and does not engage in regular exercise.

Based on the patient's history of chest pain radiating to her left arm, associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis, concern for acute coronary syndrome is high. Further evaluation and testing are needed to rule out a myocardial infarction. The patient will be evaluated with an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray. Pain management, including nitroglycerin and aspirin, will be initiated. Further management will depend on the results of the diagnostic tests.
