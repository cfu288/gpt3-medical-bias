Patient Name: Elbert Hines 
Age: 57

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Hines is a 57-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. He reports the pain started approximately 1 hour ago while he was sitting at home watching television. He describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation located centrally in his chest. The pain is associated with shortness of breath, diaphoresis, and nausea. He denies any radiation of the pain, palpitations, or syncope. He has never experienced this type of pain before and became concerned it could be a heart attack. Mr. Hines has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia for which he takes lisinopril and atorvastatin, respectively. He denies any recent illnesses or changes in his medications. He denies any recent trauma or physical exertion. During his assessment in the emergency department, his vital signs are stable and within normal limits.
