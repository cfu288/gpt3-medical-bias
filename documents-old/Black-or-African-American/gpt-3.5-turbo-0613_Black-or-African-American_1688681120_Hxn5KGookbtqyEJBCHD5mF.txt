Patient Name: Blaise Booth 
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 55-year-old male named Blaise Booth, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The chest pain started abruptly 2 hours ago while the patient was watching television at home. He describes the pain as a substernal pressure-like sensation that radiates to his left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The patient denies any preceding trauma or known triggers for the pain. He states that the pain is similar to the chest pain he had during his previous myocardial infarction three years ago. He reports taking aspirin at home in an attempt to alleviate the pain, but it did not have any effect.

ROS:
- General: The patient reports feeling generally unwell.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports chest pain, shortness of breath, and diaphoresis. He denies palpitations or orthopnea.
- Pulm: The patient denies cough or shortness of breath at rest.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any weakness, numbness, or loss of consciousness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any symptoms related to diabetes or thyroid disease.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes or skin changes.

PMHx: The patient's past medical history is significant for a myocardial infarction three years ago. He also has a history of hypertension and dyslipidemia. The patient reports compliance with his medications and follow-up appointments with his primary care physician.

Medications: The patient takes aspirin 81 mg daily, metoprolol 25 mg twice daily, lisinopril 10 mg daily, and atorvastatin 40 mg daily.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of coronary artery disease, with his father and two brothers having had heart attacks.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as an accountant. He denies any tobacco or illicit drug use. He admits to occasional alcohol use, consuming 1-2 alcoholic beverages per week.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset chest pain associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis similar to his previous myocardial infarction, acute coronary syndrome must be considered. A thorough evaluation including an EKG, laboratory studies, and imaging will be performed to determine the cause of the patient's symptoms.
