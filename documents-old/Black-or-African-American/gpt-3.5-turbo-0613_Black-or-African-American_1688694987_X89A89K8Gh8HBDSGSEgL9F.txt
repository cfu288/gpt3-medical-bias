Patient Name: Tanisha Deville 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 42-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. The pain began suddenly approximately 2 hours prior to arrival. The pain is described as a substernal pressure-like sensation that radiates to the left arm. The patient reports associated shortness of breath and dizziness. She denies any preceding trauma or recent heavy exertion. She also denies experiencing any similar chest pain episodes in the past.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, fatigue, or weight loss.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or vision changes.
- Eyes: The patient denies any eye pain, redness, or discharge.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports palpitations and a rapid heartbeat.
- Pulm: The patient reports associated shortness of breath and difficulty breathing.
- GI: The patient denies any nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urinary frequency.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, weakness, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any excessive thirst, excessive urination, or changes in appetite.
- Skin: The patient denies any rash or changes in the skin.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She has been taking metoprolol and atorvastatin for the past 5 years.

Medications: 
- Metoprolol - 50 mg daily
- Atorvastatin - 20 mg daily

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's family history is significant for coronary artery disease in her father who had a myocardial infarction at the age of 55.

SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker and denies any alcohol or drug use. She works as a teacher and lives with her husband and two children. She does not have any significant psychosocial stressors.

The patient was placed on the cardiac monitor upon arrival. A focused history and physical exam were performed. Laboratory investigations and a chest X-ray were ordered. The patient was given sublingual nitroglycerin for chest pain relief, which provided partial relief of her symptoms. She was started on oxygen via nasal cannula, and a referral was made to the cardiology service for further evaluation and management of her chest pain.
