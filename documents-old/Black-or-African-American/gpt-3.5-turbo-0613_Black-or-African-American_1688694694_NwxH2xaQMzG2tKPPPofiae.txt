HPI: The patient, Ms. Elana Walker, is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a tightness and pressure in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago while she was at rest. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or palpitations. She states that she has never experienced chest pain like this before and is concerned that it might be a heart attack. She does not have a history of cardiovascular disease.

Based on the patient's presentation of chest pain radiating to the left arm and her risk factors for cardiovascular disease, including her age and family history, it is essential to rule out cardiac causes for her symptoms.
