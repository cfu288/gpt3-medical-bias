CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Elvis Oliver, a 45-year-old male, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started suddenly about 3 hours ago while he was at rest. He describes it as a sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of his chest. He rates the pain intensity as 8 out of 10 and states that it has not improved with rest or change in position. The pain is not pleuritic and does not worsen with deep breaths or coughing. Mr. Oliver reports no cough, fever, or recent illness. He denies any history of similar episodes in the past. He has no significant medical history and denies any medications or illicit drug use.
