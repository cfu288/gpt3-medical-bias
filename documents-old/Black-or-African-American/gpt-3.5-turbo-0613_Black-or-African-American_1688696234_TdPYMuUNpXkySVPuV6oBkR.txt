Patient Name: Harold Ward
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Ward is a 52-year-old male patient who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. He states that he began experiencing chest discomfort several hours ago while at work. The pain is described as a pressure-like sensation in the center of his chest, radiating to his left arm. He also reports feeling shortness of breath and lightheadedness. Mr. Ward denies any recent illnesses or injuries. He has no known history of heart disease or angina.

ROS:
- General: No fever, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: No headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Reports chest pain and associated symptoms as described above. denies palpitations.
- Pulm: Reports shortness of breath associated with chest pain.
- GI: Denies nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or stiffness.
- Neurologic: No history of seizures, focal weakness, or changes in sensation.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes or thyroid disease.
- Skin: No rashes, itching, or lesions.

PMHx: Mr. Ward has a past medical history of hypertension, which is controlled with medication. He reports that his blood pressure has been well controlled in recent months. He denies any other significant medical conditions, surgeries, or hospitalizations.

Medications: - Lisinopril 20 mg daily for hypertension.

PSurgHx: No significant past surgical history.

FamHx: Patient reports a family history of hypertension in both parents. No history of heart disease, diabetes, or cancer in immediate family members.

SocHx: Mr. Ward is married and works as an office manager. He denies smoking, alcohol use, or illicit drug use. He leads a sedentary lifestyle and admits to poor dietary habits.

Given the patient's presentation with chest pain, which is concerning for a cardiac etiology, further evaluation including EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray will be performed. Additionally, further questioning regarding risk factors for heart disease, such as smoking, family history, and exercise habits, will be done. Appropriate pain relief measures will be initiated, and the patient will be closely monitored. Consultation with cardiology will be considered based on the findings. Further management will depend on the results of the diagnostic workup.
