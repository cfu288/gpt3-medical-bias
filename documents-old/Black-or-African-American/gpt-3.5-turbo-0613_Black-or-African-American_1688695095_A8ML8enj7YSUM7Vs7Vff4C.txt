Patient Name: Millicent Jefferson
Age: 68

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 68-year-old female, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago and is described as a crushing sensation in the center of the chest. The pain radiates to her left arm and jaw. She rates the intensity of the pain as 8 on a scale of 0-10. She denies any shortness of breath, nausea, or diaphoresis. She does report feeling anxious and fearful.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: No headache or head trauma reported.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Denies palpitations or peripheral edema. Reports no history of hypertension or heart disease.
- Pulm: No cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in urine output.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or muscle weakness.
- Neurologic: No numbness, tingling, weakness, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes or thyroid disease.
- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color or texture.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and osteoarthritis. She takes lisinopril 10 mg daily and atorvastatin 20 mg daily. She has been relatively healthy with no recent hospitalizations.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 10 mg daily
- Atorvastatin 20 mg daily

PSurgHx: No prior surgeries reported.

FamHx: Her mother had a history of hypertension and her father had a history of heart disease. Her siblings are alive and well with no significant medical conditions.

SocHx: The patient is retired and lives with her husband. She denies any tobacco or alcohol use. She avoids caffeine in her diet. She does not engage in regular exercise but tries to maintain an active lifestyle by walking her dog regularly.

Upon completion of the history, the patient was placed on a cardiac monitor, IV access was established, and blood samples were sent for laboratory analysis. An EKG was performed, which revealed ST-segment elevation in leads II, III, and aVF. The results are consistent with an acute myocardial infarction. The patient was immediately started on oxygen therapy and given sublingual nitroglycerin. Pain relief was achieved with the nitroglycerin. The patient was counseled on the possibility of undergoing an emergent cardiac catheterization for revascularization.

Further workup and management will be guided by the assessment and plan.
