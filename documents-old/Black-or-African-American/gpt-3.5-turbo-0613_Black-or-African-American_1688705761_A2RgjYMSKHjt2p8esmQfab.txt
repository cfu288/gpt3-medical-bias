Patient Name: Shelby Johnson 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Shelby Johnson, is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She reports that the pain began approximately 2 hours ago and is located substernally. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation that radiates to her left arm. The pain is rated as 8 out of 10 in intensity and is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any nausea, vomiting, or palpitations. The patient states that the pain is exacerbated by exertion but improves with rest.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or head injury.
- Eyes: The patient denies any vision changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports chest pain, shortness of breath, and diaphoresis.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, sputum production, or hemoptysis.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: The patient denies any dysuria, frequency, urgency, or hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports radiating left arm pain associated with the chest pain.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any dizziness, syncope, numbness, or weakness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any polyuria, polydipsia, or heat intolerance.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, itchiness, or lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She has been on lisinopril and atorvastatin for the past 5 years. She is up to date with her vaccinations and has no known allergies.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 10 mg daily
- Atorvastatin 20 mg daily

PSurgHx: The patient has no history of any surgical procedures.

FamHx: The patient's father had a myocardial infarction at the age of 55. Her mother is alive and has a history of hypertension and diabetes.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies any illicit drug use. She drinks alcohol socially and denies any recent travel or exposures.

Based on the patient's current presentation of chest pain, shortness of breath, and diaphoresis, with risk factors including hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and a positive family history of myocardial infarction, an acute coronary syndrome is suspected. Further evaluation with an electrocardiogram, cardiac enzymes, and chest radiograph will be undertaken to assess for any ischemic changes or evidence of cardiopulmonary pathology.
