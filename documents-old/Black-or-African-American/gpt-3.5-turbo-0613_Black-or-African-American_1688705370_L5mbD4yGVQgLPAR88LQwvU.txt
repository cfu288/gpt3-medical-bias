Patient Name: Reginald Stevens 
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: Mr. Stevens is a 55-year-old male who presents to the emergency department complaining of chest pain. He reports that the pain started abruptly about an hour ago while he was resting at home. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. The pain is associated with sweating and shortness of breath. He denies any alleviating or aggravating factors. He has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned that it may be related to his heart. He denies any history of trauma or recent illness.

- Cardiovascular: Reports palpitations and sensations of skipped beats. Denies history of heart attack or heart disease.
- Pulm: No cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: Denies abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.

- Neurologic: Denies numbness, weakness, or changes in coordination.

PMHx:

Medications: 
- Metoprolol tartrate 50mg daily

Given the patient's age, risk factors, and presentation of chest pain radiating to the left arm associated with sweating and shortness of breath, he is at high risk for acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation and appropriate management are warranted including an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and possibly a stress test.
