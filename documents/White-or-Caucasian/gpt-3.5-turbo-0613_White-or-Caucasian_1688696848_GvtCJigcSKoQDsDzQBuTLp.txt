Patient Name: Stefano Brown 
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Stefano Brown, a 52-year-old male presents to the emergency department with complaints of severe chest pain. The pain started suddenly today while he was at work. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest. The pain radiates to his left arm and jaw. He rates the pain as 9/10 and states it is not relieved by rest or nitroglycerin. He denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting.

- Pulm: Denies cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: Denies abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: Denies dysuria, hematuria, or urinary frequency.

- Neurologic: Denies numbness, weakness, or changes in sensation.

PMHx: No significant past medical history reported.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries reported.
FamHx: No known family history of coronary artery disease or other cardiac conditions.
SocHx: The patient is employed as a construction worker. He denies tobacco, alcohol, or substance abuse. He is married and lives with his wife and two adult children. He has no significant psychosocial stressors.

Pending further evaluation and assessment, the patient's chief complaint of severe chest pain raises concerns for acute myocardial infarction. Initial management includes obtaining an EKG and cardiac enzymes. Chest pain protocol and cardiac monitoring have been initiated. The patient is currently stable and will be closely monitored. Further assessments and interventions will be determined based on EKG and cardiac enzyme results.

Further note: the patient was diagnosed with an acute ST-segment elevation myocardial infarction and underwent percutaneous coronary intervention. He was transferred to the cardiopulmonary unit for further management.
