Patient Name: Annie Berg
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Ms. Berg is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started suddenly approximately one hour ago while she was at home. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of her chest, radiating to her left arm. The pain is accompanied by shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any alleviating or exacerbating factors. She has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned that it may be related to her heart.

ROS:
- General: Denies fever, fatigue, weight loss, or malaise.
- Head: Denies headache, dizziness, or trauma.
- Eyes: Denies visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Reports chest pain and shortness of breath.
- Pulm: Reports shortness of breath.
- GI: Denies nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, or changes in appetite or bowel habits.
- GU: Denies dysuria, frequency, urgency, or changes in urine color.
- Musculoskeletal: Reports pain radiating to the left arm.
- Neurologic: Denies headache, syncope, seizures, or focal weakness.
- Endocrine: No symptoms of hyperglycemia or hypoglycemia.
- Skin: Denies rash, itching, or changes in pigmentation.

PMHx: Patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. She takes lisinopril 20 mg daily and simvastatin 40 mg daily for these conditions. She denies any known allergies.

Medications: Lisinopril 20 mg daily, simvastatin 40 mg daily.

PSurgHx: No significant past surgical history.

FamHx: No family history of cardiovascular disease, diabetes, or cancer.

SocHx: Patient is a non-smoker, denies alcohol consumption, and is married with two children. She works as a teacher.

Given the patient's symptoms of sudden onset chest pain, radiating to the left arm, with associated shortness of breath and diaphoresis, concern for myocardial infarction is high. The patient will be immediately placed on a cardiac monitor, and an EKG and troponin level will be obtained. Oxygen supplementation will be initiated and intravenous access will be obtained. Further management will be determined based on the results of the initial evaluation.
