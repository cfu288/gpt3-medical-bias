Patient Name: Franklin Burgess 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Burgess is a 45-year-old male presenting to the emergency department with sudden onset of chest pain. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest, radiating to his left arm. The pain began approximately 2 hours ago while he was at home watching TV. He denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. He states that the pain has not improved with rest and is currently at a 7/10 level. He has no history of similar episodes in the past.

ROS:
- General: No fever, chills, or weight loss
- Head: No headache or dizziness
- Eyes: No visual changes or double vision
- Cardiovascular: Denies palpitations or edema
- Pulm: No cough or shortness of breath
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain
- GU: No dysuria or hematuria
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling
- Neurologic: No numbness, tingling, or weakness
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance
- Skin: No rash or skin lesions

PMHx: Patient reports a past medical history of hypertension, which is well-controlled on lisinopril. He denies any other chronic medical conditions, surgeries, or hospitalizations.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg once daily

PSurgHx: No prior surgeries

FamHx: Mother has a history of hypertension. Father passed away from a myocardial infarction at the age of 55. No other significant family history.

SocHx: Patient is married and works as an accountant. He denies tobacco or illicit drug use. Reports drinking alcohol socially on weekends.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset chest pain radiating to the left arm and associated with a crushing sensation, a myocardial infarction is a concern. A thorough cardiac workup including an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and chest x-ray will be performed. Pain management with nitroglycerin and aspirin will be initiated. Close monitoring of vital signs will be maintained. A consultation with cardiology will be requested for further evaluation and management.
