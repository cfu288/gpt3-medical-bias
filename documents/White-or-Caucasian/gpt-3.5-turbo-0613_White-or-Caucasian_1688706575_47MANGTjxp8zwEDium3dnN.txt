Patient Name: Beryl Grubbs 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 58-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She states that the pain started suddenly about 1 hour ago while she was at rest. She describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. She also reports shortness of breath and diaphoresis. There is no history of trauma or recent illness. She denies any associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or lightheadedness.

ROS:
- General: Patient is otherwise healthy and denies any weight loss or fatigue.
- Head: No headache or head trauma reported.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Patient reports chest pain and shortness of breath.
- Pulm: Denies cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath at rest.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or flank pain.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or stiffness.
- Neurologic: Patient denies any headaches, dizziness, or numbness.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes or thyroid disease.
- Skin: No rashes or skin changes.

PMHx: Patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and osteoarthritis.
Medications: Lisinopril, simvastatin, and ibuprofen as needed for arthritis pain.
PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.
FamHx: No significant family history of cardiovascular disease.
SocHx: Patient denies smoking or alcohol use. She works as a secretary and lives with her husband.

Given the patient's age, presentation of sudden-onset chest pain, radiating to the left arm, and associated symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis, a high suspicion for acute coronary syndrome is present. Further evaluation with an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray will guide management.
