Patient Name: Luc Caya

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 54-year-old male presenting to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation located in the center of his chest. The pain began approximately one hour ago and has been constant since then. The patient reports that the pain radiates to his left arm and is associated with shortness of breath. He denies any diaphoresis or nausea. There was no specific triggering event or relieving factors. The patient has a history of hypertension and has been compliant with his medication regimen.

- Head: No headache or dizziness.

- Cardiovascular: Chest pain as mentioned in the HPI. No palpitations or edema.

- GU: No dysuria or hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling.

- Endocrine: No polyuria or polydipsia.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension and is on lisinopril 10 mg once daily. He has no known allergies.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg once daily.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a myocardial infarction at age 65. His mother has a history of hypertension.

SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. He is married and works as an accountant.

Based on the patient's history of chest pain, associated symptoms of shortness of breath and radiation to the left arm, and risk factors including hypertension and family history of myocardial infarction, acute myocardial infarction is a concern. The patient will be further evaluated with an EKG, cardiac enzyme levels, and a chest X-ray to help determine the etiology of his chest pain. Pain management, oxygen supplementation, and close monitoring will be initiated while waiting for further investigation results. Cardiology consult will be obtained for further management.
