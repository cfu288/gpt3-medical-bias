CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Edmonds is a 44-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. He reports that the pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago while he was watching TV at home. He describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. The pain is persistent but worsens with activity. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. Mr. Edmonds states that he has a history of hypertension and high cholesterol but is unsure of the exact details. He states that he takes medication for these conditions but cannot recall the medications' names. He denies any recent illnesses or changes in his medications. He denies any allergies.

Mr. Edmonds will be monitored closely and cardiology consulted for further evaluation and management.
