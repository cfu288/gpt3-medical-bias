Patient Name: Clinton Jennings 
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Jennings is a 55-year-old male presenting to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. He reports that the pain started approximately 2 hours ago while he was at home watching television. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. He rates the pain as 8 out of 10 in intensity. He also reports associated symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis. He denies any previous episodes of chest pain or similar symptoms.

ROS:

- Head: The patient denies any headache, dizziness, or trauma.

- Pulm: The patient reports a history of occasional cough but denies any recent respiratory symptoms such as coughing up blood, wheezing, or shortness of breath at rest.

- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms.

- Endocrine: The patient denies any history of diabetes, thyroid disease, or hormonal imbalances.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He reports taking daily medications for these conditions but is unable to provide an accurate list at this time.

Medications: The patient is unsure of the names and dosages of his current medications.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. His father had a heart attack in his 60s.

SocHx: The patient used to smoke but quit 10 years ago. He denies any alcohol or recreational drug use. He is currently unemployed and lives alone.

The patient's symptoms of chest pain along with associated shortness of breath and diaphoresis are concerning for a cardiac etiology. Given the patient's history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and family history of heart disease, further evaluation with an electrocardiogram and cardiac enzymes is warranted to rule out a myocardial infarction.
