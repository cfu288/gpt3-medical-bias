Patient Name: Barrie Holloran 
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Holloran is a 52-year-old male presenting to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. The pain began suddenly approximately 2 hours prior to arrival and is described as a crushing pressure in the center of the chest. The pain radiates to both arms and is associated with shortness of breath. The patient denies any previous episodes of chest pain and states that the pain is unrelieved by rest. He also reports feeling nauseous and lightheaded.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies fever, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: No history of headaches or head trauma.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Positive for chest pain, shortness of breath, and radiation of pain to both arms. Negative for palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: Positive for shortness of breath, negative for cough or wheezing.
- GI: Positive for nausea, negative for vomiting or abdominal pain.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes.
- Musculoskeletal: Negative for joint pain or muscle weakness.
- Neurologic: Negative for headaches, dizziness, or numbness/weakness.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes or thyroid disease.
- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or skin changes noted.

PMHx: Patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He is not currently taking any medications and does not have his previous medical records available.

Medications: None reported.

PSurgHx: No previous surgical history.

FamHx: Patient reports no significant family history of cardiovascular disease, but his father passed away from a heart attack at the age of 68.

SocHx: Patient is employed as a construction worker. He denies tobacco or illicit drug use but drinks alcohol occasionally.

Given the patient's age, sudden onset of severe chest pain with radiation, and associated symptoms of shortness of breath, nausea, and lightheadedness, the possibility of an acute coronary syndrome is concerning. This patient should be evaluated urgently to rule out a cardiac cause for his symptoms.
