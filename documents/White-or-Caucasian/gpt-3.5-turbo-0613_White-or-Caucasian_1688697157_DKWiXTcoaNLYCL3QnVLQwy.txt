Patient Name: Christy Bourgeois
Age: 48

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 48-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. Patient states that the pain started approximately 2 hours ago while she was at home. She describes the pain as a sudden, sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of her chest. She rates the severity of the pain as a 7 out of 10. The pain is constant and does not radiate to any other areas. The patient denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, palpitations, nausea, vomiting, diaphoresis, or lightheadedness. She also denies any history of similar episodes in the past. The patient states that she has tried taking over-the-counter pain medications, but they have not provided any relief. She is concerned about the possibility of a heart attack and came to the ED for evaluation.

- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, itching, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity.
Medications: Amlodipine 5mg daily, Atorvastatin 20mg daily.
PSurgHx: The patient has no history of any surgeries.
