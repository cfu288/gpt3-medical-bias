Patient Name: Meaghan Jones 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mrs. Jones is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with chest pain. She reports that the pain started about an hour ago while she was at home watching TV. She describes the pain as a squeezing pressure in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain has been constant and has not improved with rest or position changes. She denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. She has never experienced this type of pain before.

ROS:

- Head: No headache or head injury.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: As described above.
- Pulm: No cough, sputum production, or shortness of breath.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: No dysuria or hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No dizziness or paresthesias.
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat intolerance.
- Skin: No rash or lesions.

PMHx: Mrs. Jones has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She was diagnosed with hypertension 5 years ago and is currently on amlodipine 5 mg daily. She was diagnosed with hyperlipidemia 3 years ago and is on atorvastatin 20 mg daily.

Medications: 
1. Amlodipine 5 mg daily for hypertension.
2. Atorvastatin 20 mg daily for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: No family history of heart disease or stroke. Mother is alive with history of hypertension. Father died of a stroke at age 62.

SocHx: Mrs. Jones is married and works as an accountant. She is a non-smoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She exercises regularly and follows a healthy diet.

Chest pain in a middle-aged woman with risk factors for cardiovascular disease raises concern for an acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation and diagnostic workup are necessary to confirm the diagnosis and determine the appropriate management plan.
