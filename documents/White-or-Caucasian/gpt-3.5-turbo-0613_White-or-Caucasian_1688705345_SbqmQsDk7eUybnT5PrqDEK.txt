Patient Name: Abel Baum 
Age: 45 

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Baum is a 45-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The patient states that he woke up this morning with a sharp, squeezing pain in the center of his chest. He describes the pain as a 9/10 in severity and states that it radiates down his left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The patient denies any previous episodes of chest pain, and states that this is the first time he has experienced this type of pain. He also denies any recent trauma or fevers. 

ROS:
- General: The patient reports feeling generally unwell and fatigued over the past few weeks.
- Head: No headache or head injury reported.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain reported.
- Cardiovascular: The patient denies any palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: The patient reports occasional cough with phlegm production, but denies any chest congestion or wheezing.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain reported.
- GU: No urinary changes or pain reported.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports occasional back pain but denies any recent injuries or fractures.
- Neurologic: No numbness, tingling, or weakness reported.
- Endocrine: No recent changes in weight, thirst, or urination reported.
- Skin: No rash, lesions, or changes in skin color reported.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He denies any other significant medical conditions.

Medications: The patient takes lisinopril for hypertension and atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: The patient has no previous surgical history.

FamHx: The patient's father had a history of myocardial infarction at the age of 60. There is no other significant family history of cardiovascular disease.

SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker and denies any illicit drug use. He consumes alcohol occasionally but denies any excessive alcohol intake. He is married and works as an accountant. There are no known environmental exposures or recent travel.

Based on the patient's history and presentation of chest pain radiating down the left arm, there is concern for a possible myocardial infarction. Further evaluation with an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and imaging studies are indicated to rule out acute coronary syndrome.
