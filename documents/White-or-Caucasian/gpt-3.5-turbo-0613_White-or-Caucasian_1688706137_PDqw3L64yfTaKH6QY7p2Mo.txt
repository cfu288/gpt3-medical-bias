CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Eplin is a 48-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. The patient also reports associated shortness of breath and diaphoresis. He denies any other symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or lightheadedness.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes. He also has a history of smoking for 15 years but quit 10 years ago.

Medications: The patient takes lisinopril for hypertension, atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia, and metformin for type 2 diabetes.

Based on the patient's presentation, he is at high risk for acute coronary syndrome. Immediate intervention is required to rule out a myocardial infarction. The plan includes obtaining an EKG, troponin levels, and initiating appropriate management for his chest pain.
