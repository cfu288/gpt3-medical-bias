Patient Name: Harvey Emerick 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Emerick is a 58-year-old male presenting to the emergency department with acute-onset chest pain. He reports that the pain began approximately 2 hours ago while he was at rest. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of his chest, radiating to his left arm. He rates the pain as an 8 out of 10 in severity. He denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. He also denies any recent illness, injuries, or known cardiac history.

ROS:
- General: No fever, chills, or weight changes.
- Head: No headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Chest pain as described above. No palpitations or dyspnea.
- Pulm: No cough, sputum production, or difficulty breathing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in urinary output.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No focal weakness, numbness, or changes in sensation.
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes or changes in skin color.

PMHx: No significant past medical history. Patient reports he is generally healthy and has not been hospitalized before.
Medications: Patient denies taking any prescription or over-the-counter medications.
PSurgHx: No previous surgeries or procedures.
FamHx: Patient reports that his father had a myocardial infarction at age 63. No other significant family medical history.
SocHx: Patient is an ex-smoker, having quit 5 years ago after smoking a pack a day for 30 years. Denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. Works as an office accountant and denies any significant occupational exposures.

Based on the patient's presenting symptoms of acute-onset chest pain radiating to the left arm, myocardial infarction should be strongly considered. Other differentials to be ruled out include angina, pericarditis, and esophageal reflux. Cardiac biomarkers, EKG, chest x-ray, and further evaluation will be necessary to determine the cause of the patient's chest pain.
