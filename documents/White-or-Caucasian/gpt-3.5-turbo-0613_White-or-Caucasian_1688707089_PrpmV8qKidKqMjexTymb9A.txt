Patient Name: April Walker 
Age: 43

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 43-year-old female named April Walker, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She reports that the pain started suddenly approximately 2 hours ago while she was at home watching television. She describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation localized to the center of her chest. The pain is radiating to her left arm and jaw. She rates the pain as 8 out of 10 in intensity and states that it has not improved with rest. She denies any associated shortness of breath, dizziness, nausea, or diaphoresis.

- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or blurred vision.

- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urinary habits.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports radiating pain to the left arm, but denies any joint pain, swelling, or limitations in movement.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any headache, dizziness, syncope, numbness, or weakness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any symptoms related to the endocrine system.
- Skin: The patient denies any rash, itching, or changes in skin color.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg daily.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgical procedures.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension in both parents. No other significant family medical history.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies alcohol or illicit drug use. She works as a nurse in a local hospital and is currently divorced.

- Blood pressure: 140/90 mmHg
- Heart rate: 90 beats per minute
- Respiratory rate: 16 breaths per minute
