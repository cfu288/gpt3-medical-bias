Patient Name: Elwood Fearn 
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. The pain is described as a pressure-like sensation, radiating to the left arm and jaw. The pain began suddenly approximately 2 hours ago while the patient was at rest. The patient states that the pain is severe and has not improved with rest or over-the-counter pain medications. He denies any associated shortness of breath, nausea, vomiting, or diaphoresis. The patient has a history of hypertension and is currently on medication for it.

The patient's presentation, with sudden onset severe chest pain and associated symptoms radiating to the left arm and jaw, is concerning for acute coronary syndrome. Given the patient's age, male gender, and history of hypertension, it is important to rule out a cardiac etiology. Further evaluation including an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and further risk stratification will be initiated.
