Patient Name: Inge Allen 
Age: 56

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: Inge Allen is a 56-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She reports experiencing sudden onset sharp chest pain that radiates to her left arm. The pain started approximately two hours ago while she was resting at home. She describes the pain as severe and rates it 9/10 on the pain scale. The pain is accompanied by shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or lightheadedness.

- Head: No headache or head injury.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports a history of hypertension but denies any palpitations or previous heart problems.
- Pulm: Denies cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath at rest.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in urinary habits.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain, swelling, or difficulty with mobility.
- Neurologic: No focal weakness, numbness, or changes in sensation.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes or thyroid diseases.
- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or unusual skin changes.

PMHx: The patient has a medical history significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She is on medications for both conditions, which include lisinopril and atorvastatin.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg daily, atorvastatin 20 mg daily.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension and heart disease in her parents.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies alcohol or illicit drug use. She works as a teacher and is currently in a monogamous relationship. She lives with her partner and they have two adult children.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset severe chest pain radiating to the left arm, along with symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis, the primary concern is an acute coronary syndrome. Emergency department evaluation will include further assessment of the patient's cardiac status, including an EKG and cardiac enzymes. Pain control will also be addressed.
