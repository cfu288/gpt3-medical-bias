Patient Name: Santo Sturgis 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient is a 42-year-old male presenting to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago while the patient was at rest. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. The patient rates the pain as 8/10 in severity. He denies any shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. He admits to a history of smoking 1 pack per day for the past 20 years and having a sedentary lifestyle. There is no history of similar symptoms in the past. 

ROS:
- General: The patient denies fever, chills, and weight loss.
- Head: The patient denies headache and head trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies visual changes and eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient complains of chest pain as noted above. No palpitations or edema reported.
- Pulm: The patient denies cough, wheezing, and shortness of breath.
- GI: The patient denies abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies dysuria and hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports the pain radiating to his left arm but denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies dizziness, syncope, and numbness or weakness in any extremities.
- Endocrine: The patient denies polyuria, polydipsia, and polyphagia.
- Skin: The patient denies skin changes and any rashes.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension diagnosed 5 years ago and is currently taking lisinopril. No other significant medical history reported.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 10 mg daily for hypertension

PSurgHx: No significant surgical history reported.

FamHx: The patient's father had a myocardial infarction at the age of 60. There is no family history of other significant medical conditions.

SocHx: The patient is employed as an office manager. He is married and lives with his spouse. He has a sedentary lifestyle and admits to smoking 1 pack of cigarettes per day for the past 20 years. He denies alcohol and illicit drug use. The patient has no known allergies.

1. Chest pain is concerning for possible acute coronary syndrome given the location, radiation, and severity of the pain.
2. Patient's smoking history and sedentary lifestyle are risk factors for cardiovascular disease.
3. Obtain ECG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest x-ray to evaluate for possible acute myocardial infarction or other cardiac pathology.
4. Administer sublingual nitroglycerin and aspirin for possible ischemic chest pain.
5. Consider admission to the chest pain unit for further evaluation and management.
