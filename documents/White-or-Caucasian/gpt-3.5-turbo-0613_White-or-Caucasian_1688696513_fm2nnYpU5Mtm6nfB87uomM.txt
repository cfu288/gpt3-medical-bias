Patient Name: Bryan Bradfield
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Bradfield, is a 42-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started approximately 2 hours ago while he was at home. He describes the pain as a dull, pressure-like sensation that radiates to his left arm. The pain is worsening with exertion and is relieved by rest. He denies any shortness of breath, palpitations, diaphoresis, or dizziness. He reports that he has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned it may be related to his heart.

ROS:
- General: The patient reports no weight loss, fatigue, or fever.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies any changes in vision, eye pain, or redness.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports the aforementioned chest pain but denies any palpitations or irregular heartbeat.
- Pulm: The patient denies any shortness of breath, cough, wheezing, or sputum production.
- GI: The patient denies any nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urinary habits.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports radiating pain to his left arm but denies any joint pain or limited mobility.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any headaches, dizziness, syncope, weakness, or numbness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any changes in appetite, thirst, or urination.
- Skin: The patient denies any rash, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient reports a history of hypertension, which is well-controlled with lisinopril. He also reports a past medical history of hyperlipidemia, for which he takes atorvastatin. He denies any other chronic medical conditions or hospitalizations.

Medications: Lisinopril 20 mg daily, atorvastatin 40 mg daily

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension in his mother and a history of coronary artery disease in his father, who had a myocardial infarction at age 55.

SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker and denies any significant alcohol consumption or illicit drug use. He works as an accountant and leads a sedentary lifestyle.

Based on the patient's history and presenting symptoms, further evaluation and diagnostic testing, including an electrocardiogram (EKG) and cardiac enzymes, will be initiated to assess for a possible cardiac etiology of his chest pain.
