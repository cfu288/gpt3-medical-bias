Patient Name: Katarzyna Ciriello 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient is a 42-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She reports that the pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago while she was at home watching television. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the middle of her chest, radiating to her left arm. The pain is constant and is not relieved with rest. She rates the pain as 7/10 in severity. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. She denies any previous history of similar episodes.

ROS:
- General: No fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: No headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: No changes in vision, eye pain, or eye discharge.
- Cardiovascular: As mentioned above, the patient is experiencing chest pain that radiates to her left arm.
- Pulm: No cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: No dysuria, frequency, urgency, or hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: No extremity pain or joint swelling.
- Neurologic: No headache, dizziness, or focal neurological symptoms.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes or thyroid disease.
- Skin: No rash or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and hyperlipidemia. She also has a history of smoking for 10 years but quit 5 years ago.

Medications: Lisinopril for hypertension, metformin for diabetes, and atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension and coronary artery disease in her parents.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as a nurse in a local hospital. She denies any history of illicit drug use. She does not consume alcohol regularly but reports occasional social drinking.

Based on the patient's chief complaint, history of sudden onset chest pain radiating to the left arm, and risk factors such as smoking, hypertension, and diabetes, the possibility of an acute myocardial infarction should be considered. Further evaluation and workup, including an electrocardiogram, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray, will be performed. Pain management and appropriate monitoring will also be initiated.
