CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient, an otherwise healthy 45-year-old male, presents to the Emergency Department with complaints of chest pain. The pain started suddenly approximately three hours prior to arrival and has been constant since then. The patient describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of his chest, radiating to his left arm. He rates the pain as 8 out of 10 in severity. The pain is exacerbated by deep breaths and movement, and is slightly relieved by rest. The patient denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, palpitations, dizziness, or diaphoresis. He states that he has never experienced this type of pain before.
