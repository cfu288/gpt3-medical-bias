Patient Name: Ed Wasserman 
Age: 56

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Wasserman is a 56-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started abruptly about 2 hours ago while he was at home. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left shoulder and arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath. He denies any recent illness, trauma, or strenuous activity that could explain the pain. He states that the pain is the worst he has ever experienced and is not relieved with rest.

- Head: Patient denies headache or trauma.
- Eyes: Patient denies blurry vision or changes in vision.
- Cardiovascular: Patient reports chest pain and shortness of breath. No palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: Patient denies cough, sputum production, or wheezing.
- GI: Patient denies nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: Patient denies urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: Patient reports radiating pain to the left shoulder and arm.
- Neurologic: Patient denies dizziness, fainting, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: No symptoms of hormonal imbalance reported.
- Skin: No skin changes or rashes reported.

PMHx: Patient has a history of hypertension for which he takes lisinopril. He also has a history of hyperlipidemia and takes atorvastatin.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril for hypertension.
- Atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: No significant surgical history reported.

FamHx: Patient's father had a history of myocardial infarction.

SocHx: Patient is currently employed as an accountant. He denies tobacco, alcohol, or illicit drug use. He lives with his wife and has two grown children.

Based on the patient's presenting symptoms and history, further evaluation for acute coronary syndrome is warranted. EKG, cardiac biomarkers, and a chest X-ray will be ordered. The patient will be closely monitored and managed accordingly.
