HPI: The patient, a 45-year-old female, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started suddenly approximately 2 hours ago while the patient was at rest. The pain is described as a squeezing sensation in the center of her chest, radiating to her left arm and jaw. The patient rates the pain as 8/10 in intensity and states that it has not subsided since onset. The patient denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. She reports that she has never experienced chest pain like this before. Upon further questioning, the patient admits to a 30-pack-year smoking history and a family history of coronary artery disease. She does not have any history of diabetes, hypertension, or hyperlipidemia.
