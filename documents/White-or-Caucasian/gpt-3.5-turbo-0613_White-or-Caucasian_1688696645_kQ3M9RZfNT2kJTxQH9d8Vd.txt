CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mrs. Williamson is a 54-year-old female with a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The patient reports that the pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago while she was at rest. She describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The patient denies any nausea, vomiting, or syncope. She has never experienced this type of pain before.
