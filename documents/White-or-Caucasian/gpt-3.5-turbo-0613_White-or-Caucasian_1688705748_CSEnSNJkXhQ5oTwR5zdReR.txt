Patient Name: Marylee Weinstein 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 45-year-old woman named Marylee Weinstein, presents to the emergency department complaining of chest pain. The pain started suddenly about an hour ago while she was at home watching television. She describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and she feels lightheaded. There is no known trigger for the pain and it has not improved with rest. She denies any previous history of chest pain or similar symptoms. She denies any other associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diaphoresis, or palpitations. She is currently feeling anxious and concerned about the pain.

Based on the patient's history of sudden-onset chest pain, radiating to the left arm, associated with shortness of breath and lightheadedness, and not improved with rest, there is concern for a cardiac etiology. The patient will be further evaluated, including an electrocardiogram, cardiac enzyme screening, and chest radiograph, to rule out acute myocardial infarction and other possible causes of her symptoms. The patient should also be monitored for any changes in her symptoms and receive appropriate pain management.
