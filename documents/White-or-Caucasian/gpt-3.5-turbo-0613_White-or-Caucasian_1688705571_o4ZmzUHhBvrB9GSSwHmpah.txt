Patient Name: Denise Summers
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient is a 42-year-old female who presents to the Emergency Department with complaints of chest pain. She states that the pain started suddenly about 3 hours ago while she was at rest. She describes the pain as a dull, pressure-like sensation located in the center of her chest. She denies any radiation of the pain, shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or associated symptoms such as nausea or vomiting. She states that the pain has not changed in intensity since it started.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, or weight changes.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or head injury.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient denies any palpitations or lightheadedness.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough or shortness of breath.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, weakness, or confusion.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any symptoms related to diabetes or thyroid disorder.
- Skin: The patient denies any rash or skin changes.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, for which she takes lisinopril. She also has a history of dyslipidemia and is on atorvastatin. She has no known allergies.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 10 mg once daily
- Atorvastatin 20 mg once daily

PSurgHx: The patient has no prior surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's mother has a history of hypertension and her father has a history of heart disease.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies any illicit drug use. She lives with her husband and two children. She works as a teacher. There is no relevant social history to note.

Given the patient's presenting complaint of chest pain and her past medical history of hypertension and dyslipidemia, further evaluation is warranted to rule out a cardiac etiology. The patient will be further evaluated with an electrocardiogram, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray. Pain management will also be addressed and a plan will be formulated based on the results of the diagnostic tests and assessment.
