Patient Name: Heide Ferell 
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 55-year-old female who presents to the emergency department complaining of sudden-onset chest pain. The pain is described as a squeezing sensation in the center of her chest, radiating to her left arm. The pain began approximately one hour ago and has been constant since then. She denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. There is no known precipitating factor. The patient reports a history of hypertension, for which she takes Amlodipine 5 mg daily, and hyperlipidemia, for which she takes Atorvastatin 20 mg daily. She denies any previous episodes of chest pain or cardiac events. There is no history of smoking or illicit drug use. The patient is concerned that her symptoms may be indicative of a heart attack.
