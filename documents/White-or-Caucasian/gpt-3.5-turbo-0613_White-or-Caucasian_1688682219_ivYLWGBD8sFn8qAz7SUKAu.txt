Patient Name: Joseph Whicker 
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient, a 55-year-old male, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest that started approximately 3 hours ago. The pain is radiating to his left arm and is associated with shortness of breath. The patient denies any recent trauma or exertion preceding the onset of his symptoms. He has no previous history of similar episodes. 

On further questioning, the patient reports no associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diaphoresis, or lightheadedness. He is not aware of any known triggers for his chest pain. He states that the pain is constant and has not improved or worsened since onset. The patient denies any relief with rest or any aggravating factors. He has not taken any medications for the pain.

- Head: No headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: No changes in vision or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Positive for chest pain, negative for palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: Positive for shortness of breath, negative for cough or wheezing.

- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or stiffness.
- Neurologic: No weakness or numbness.
- Endocrine: No polyuria or polydipsia.
- Skin: No rashes or lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He is compliant with his medications which include lisinopril, atorvastatin, and metformin.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 10 mg daily for hypertension
- Atorvastatin 20 mg daily for hyperlipidemia
- Metformin 1000 mg twice daily for type 2 diabetes mellitus

PSurgHx: None

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of coronary artery disease in his father who had a myocardial infarction at the age of 65.

Based on the patient's presenting symptoms, medical history, and family history of coronary artery disease, there is concern for a cardiac etiology of the chest pain. Further evaluation including an EKG and cardiac enzymes will be obtained. The patient will also be monitored closely for any changes in his condition and managed accordingly.
