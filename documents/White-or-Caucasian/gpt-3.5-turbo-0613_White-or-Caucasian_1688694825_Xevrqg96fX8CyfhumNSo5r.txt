CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 48-year-old female, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. The pain is described as a squeezing sensation in the center of her chest, radiating to her left arm. She rates the pain as 8/10 in severity and states that it started suddenly while she was at rest. She denies any previous episodes of similar chest pain. There are no alleviating or aggravating factors mentioned. She also reports associated symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis. There is no history of trauma preceding the onset of symptoms.

- Pulm: The patient reports shortness of breath but denies cough or wheezing.

- Neurologic: No dizziness, syncope, or paresthesias.

Medications: The patient takes amlodipine 5mg daily for hypertension, and atorvastatin 20mg daily for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: The patient has no history of any surgical procedures.

FamHx: Family history is noncontributory. Her parents are both alive and healthy. She has no siblings.
