Patient Name: Marlyn Sexton 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Ms. Sexton, is a 58-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with chief complaint of chest pain. She reports that the pain started suddenly about 3 hours ago while she was at home watching television. She describes the pain as a burning sensation and pressure in the center of her chest. The pain is radiating to her left arm and jaw. She also reports feeling short of breath and nauseous. She denies any associated symptoms such as diaphoresis or lightheadedness. Ms. Sexton states that she has never experienced this type of pain before, and it is concerning to her.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: As mentioned in HPI, the patient reports chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, shortness of breath, and nausea. She denies palpitations and syncope.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough or shortness of breath at rest.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms such as frequency, urgency, or dysuria.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain or muscle weakness.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any headache, dizziness, numbness, or weakness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any excessive thirst, urination, or changes in appetite.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She has been taking amlodipine 10 mg daily and atorvastatin 40 mg daily for the past 5 years. She does not recall any recent changes in her medications.

Medications: 
- Amlodipine 10 mg daily
- Atorvastatin 40 mg daily

PSurgHx: The patient does not have any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a myocardial infarction at the age of 60. Her mother is alive with no significant medical history. Her brother has hypertension and diabetes.

SocHx: The patient is married and lives with her husband. She is a retired accountant and denies any tobacco or alcohol use. She exercises regularly by walking for 30 minutes every day.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, along with associated shortness of breath and nausea, there is concern for an acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation, including an EKG and cardiac enzymes, will be performed to assess for possible myocardial infarction.
