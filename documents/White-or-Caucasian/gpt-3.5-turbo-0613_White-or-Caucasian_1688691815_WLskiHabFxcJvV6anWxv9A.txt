Patient Name: Herbert Carstens 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 58-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started suddenly approximately 2 hours prior to arrival while the patient was at rest. The pain is substernal, squeezing, and radiates to the left arm. The patient rates the pain as 7/10 in severity. The patient denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or lightheadedness. He has no history of similar chest pain in the past. The patient tried taking nitroglycerin at home with no relief. Upright position seems to provide some relief. The patient denies any recent illness, trauma, or strenuous physical activity. He denies any personal or family history of heart disease.

- Head: No headache or head injury reported.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He reports taking lisinopril for hypertension and atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia. No other significant past medical history reported.

Medications: Lisinopril 20 mg once daily, atorvastatin 40 mg once daily.

PSurgHx: No significant past surgical history reported.

FamHx: No significant family history of heart disease reported.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset chest pain radiating to the left arm and unrelieved by nitroglycerin, acute coronary syndrome is a concern. Further evaluation including EKG, cardiac enzymes, and serial troponins will be performed. Pain management and cardiac monitoring will be initiated.
