Patient Name: Kristi Campos 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Kristi Campos, is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She states that the pain started approximately 2 hours ago and is located in the center of her chest. She describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation that radiates to her left arm. The pain is intermittent and has a severity of 7 out of 10. The patient denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, nausea, vomiting, or diaphoresis. She states that she has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned it may be related to her heart.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: The patient denies any headache, dizziness, or trauma to the head.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or double vision.
- Cardiovascular: As mentioned earlier, the patient is experiencing chest pain. No other symptoms such as palpitations or edema are reported.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urine color.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports the pain radiating to the left arm.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any changes in appetite, thirst, or heat intolerance.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. She is currently not on any medications for these conditions.

Medications: None reported.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of heart disease in her father who had a myocardial infarction at the age of 52.

SocHx: The patient is a current smoker with a 20 pack-year history. She denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She is married and works as a teacher.

Based on the patient's history of chest pain radiating to her left arm and her risk factors for cardiovascular disease (hypertension, hyperlipidemia, obesity, and smoking), there is concern for a possible myocardial infarction. Further evaluation and management are warranted, including EKG, cardiac enzyme levels, and possibly cardiac catheterization.
