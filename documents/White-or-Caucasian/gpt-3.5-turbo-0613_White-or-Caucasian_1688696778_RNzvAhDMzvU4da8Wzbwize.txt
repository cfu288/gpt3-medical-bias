Patient Name: Dusty Yoder

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Dusty Yoder, a 42-year-old male, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The patient reports that the pain began earlier this evening while he was watching television. He describes the pain as a sharp, squeezing sensation located in the center of his chest. The pain has been constant since it started and has not radiated to any other areas. The patient also reports experiencing shortness of breath and diaphoresis associated with the pain. He denies any cough, fever, chills, or recent illness. There is no history of trauma or recent strenuous activity preceding the onset of symptoms. The patient states that he has never experienced this type of pain before and it has prompted him to seek medical attention.
