Patient Name: Gigi Haddad 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 42-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of severe chest pain. She describes the pain as a sharp, squeezing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm and jaw. She states that the pain started about an hour ago and has been constant since then. She denies any shortness of breath, palpitations, or diaphoresis. She mentions that the pain is worse with deep breaths and movement. There was no history of trauma or fever prior to the onset of the pain. She denies any previous episodes of similar pain.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, fatigue, or weight loss.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: As mentioned earlier, the patient reports chest pain radiating to her left arm and jaw. She denies any palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough or shortness of breath.
- GI: The patient denies any nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: The patient denies any dysuria or urinary frequency.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports that the chest pain is worsened with movement.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, or weakness.
- Endocrine: There is no significant endocrine history reported.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes or skin changes.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She is not currently taking any medications for these conditions.
Medications: The patient denies any current medications.
PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.
FamHx: The patient's family history is noncontributory. No family members have a history of cardiac disease.
SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She is married and works as a nurse in a local hospital. There is no significant occupational exposure or recent travel history. She has a sedentary lifestyle and does not regularly participate in physical exercise.

The patient's symptoms raise concern for a possible cardiac etiology. Given the severity and radiation of the chest pain, it is important to rule out acute coronary syndrome. Further evaluation with an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest radiograph will be ordered to assess for any cardiac abnormalities. Pain control will also be initiated to provide relief and maximize patient comfort while awaiting further results.
