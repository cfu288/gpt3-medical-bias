Patient Name: Saul Boykin 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Boykin is a 58-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started suddenly about an hour ago and is described as a sharp, substernal chest pain that radiates to his left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath, diaphoresis, and nausea. The patient denies any prior episodes of similar chest pain. There is no recent history of trauma or recent illness. The patient reports that he has been feeling fatigued over the past few weeks and has had difficulty climbing stairs without feeling short of breath.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, fatigue, or weight changes.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports intermittent episodes of palpitations but denies any history of chest pain or shortness of breath before today's episode.
- Pulm: The patient reports occasional cough but denies any hemoptysis or shortness of breath at rest.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports occasional joint pain but denies any swelling, redness, or limitation of movement.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any weakness, numbness, or tingling.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any heat or cold intolerance, polyuria, polydipsia, or recent changes in weight.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes or skin lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension, dyslipidemia, and obesity. He has been taking lisinopril for his hypertension and atorvastatin for his dyslipidemia.

Medications: 
- Lisinopril 10 mg daily
- Atorvastatin 40 mg daily

PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a history of heart disease and underwent a coronary artery bypass graft surgery at the age of 65. His mother is alive with no known medical conditions. There is no family history of cancer, diabetes, or other hereditary conditions.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as a computer programmer. He denies tobacco, alcohol, or illicit drug use. He lives a sedentary lifestyle and does not engage in regular exercise.

Based on the patient's history of sudden onset substernal chest pain with radiation to the left arm, associated with shortness of breath, diaphoresis, and nausea, there is concern for an acute coronary syndrome. The patient presents with several risk factors for coronary artery disease, including hypertension, dyslipidemia, and obesity. Further evaluation and management are warranted, including an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and consultation with the cardiology service.
