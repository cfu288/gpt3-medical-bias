Patient Name: Hank Louvier 
Age: 57

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Louvier is a 57-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. He describes the pain as a heavy pressure in the center of his chest that started approximately two hours ago. The pain radiates down his left arm and is associated with shortness of breath. He denies any recent trauma or injury. He notes that the pain is worse with activity and improves with rest. He denies any previous episodes of similar chest pain.

ROS:
- General: No fevers, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: No headaches or head trauma.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: No palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: No cough or shortness of breath except during episodes of chest pain.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in urinary habits.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain, swelling, or stiffness.
- Neurologic: No dizziness, syncope, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes or thyroid disease.
- Skin: No rashes, itching, or changes in moles.

PMHx: Mr. Louvier has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and osteoarthritis. He denies any history of coronary artery disease, heart attacks, or other significant medical conditions.

Medications: Mr. Louvier takes hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily for his hypertension and atorvastatin 20 mg daily for his hyperlipidemia. He also takes acetaminophen as needed for his osteoarthritis pain.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: Mr. Louvier's father had a heart attack at the age of 65. His mother is alive with no significant medical history. He has two siblings who are both in good health.

SocHx: Mr. Louvier is a retired construction worker. He lives alone and denies tobacco or alcohol use. He has a sedentary lifestyle and does not participate in regular exercise.

Given the patient's age, risk factors, and presentation of chest pain radiating to the left arm, a thorough evaluation for possible acute coronary syndrome will be initiated.
