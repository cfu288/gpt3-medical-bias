Patient Name: Cinda Duvall 
Age: 56

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mrs. Duvall is a 56-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She reports that the pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago while she was at home watching television. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates down both arms. She also reports associated shortness of breath and nausea. The pain is not relieved by rest or over-the-counter pain medications. She denies any recent illness or trauma. Upon further questioning, she reveals a history of hypertension and a family history of cardiovascular disease.

- Head: No headache or head injury

- Cardiovascular: Chest pain, shortness of breath
- Pulm: No cough or sputum production

- Neurologic: No weakness or numbness
- Endocrine: No polyuria or polydipsia
- Skin: No rashes or lesions

PMHx:

Medications:

PSurgHx: 
- None

- Mother had a heart attack at the age of 60

SocHx:

Based on the patient's history of sudden-onset chest pain radiating down both arms, associated shortness of breath and nausea, and a family history of cardiovascular disease, the differential diagnosis includes acute coronary syndrome, pulmonary embolism, and aortic dissection. Immediate evaluation with an electrocardiogram, laboratory tests including cardiac enzymes, and a chest x-ray is warranted.
