Patient Name: Florence Crawford

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 54-year-old female named Florence Crawford, presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. She reports that the pain started suddenly approximately 2 hours ago while she was at home. She describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation in the center of her chest with radiation to her left arm. The pain is constant and has not subsided since onset. She denies any alleviating or exacerbating factors. She also reports associated symptoms of shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The patient has no history of similar episodes in the past. She denies any recent illnesses, injuries, or changes in medication.

The patient's presenting complaint, chest pain radiating to the left arm, associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis, raises concern for a cardiac etiology. Given her risk factors including hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes, further evaluation including an electrocardiogram, cardiac enzyme studies, and a chest x-ray will be warranted. Pain management and risk stratification will guide further management.
