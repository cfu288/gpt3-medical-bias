Patient Name: Melvin Bellinger
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: Mr. Bellinger is a 52-year-old male who presents to the Emergency Department with complaints of sudden onset severe chest pain. Patient states that the pain started about an hour ago while he was at work. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the middle of his chest, radiating to his left arm. He rates the pain as a 9/10 and states that it is not relieved with rest. He denies any shortness of breath, nausea, or diaphoresis.

ROS:
- General: Patient denies any fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: No headaches or dizziness reported.
- Eyes: No vision changes or blurred vision.
- Cardiovascular: Patient complains of chest pain, denies palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: No cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: No urinary frequency, urgency, or burning sensation.
- Musculoskeletal: Patient reports chest pain and left arm pain.
- Neurologic: No numbness, tingling, weakness, or changes in coordination reported.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes, thyroid disorder, or adrenal disease.
- Skin: No rashes, skin lesions, or wounds.

PMHx: Patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and a previous myocardial infarction 5 years ago. He also has a family history of coronary artery disease.

Medications: Patient is currently taking lisinopril, atorvastatin, and aspirin.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries reported.

FamHx: Family history is significant for coronary artery disease, with his father and two brothers having heart attacks in their 60s.

SocHx: Patient is a non-smoker and denies alcohol or illicit drug use. He works as a construction worker and denies any recent stressors.

Based on the patient's history and presentation, a thorough cardiac workup is indicated to rule out acute coronary syndrome.
