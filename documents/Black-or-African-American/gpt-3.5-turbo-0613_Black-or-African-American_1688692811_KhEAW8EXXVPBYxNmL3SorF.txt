Patient Name: Tyrone Sandidge
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Sandidge, is a 42-year-old male who presents to the emergency department today with complaints of chest pain. He reports that the pain began approximately 2 hours ago while he was resting at home. The pain is described as a sharp, squeezing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. Mr. Sandidge also reports feeling short of breath and diaphoretic. He denies any associated symptoms such as nausea or vomiting. He states that he has never experienced this type of pain before.

ROS:
- General: The patient reports feeling generally unwell and fatigued recently.
- Head: The patient denies any headaches or head trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies any vision changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports chest pain as described above. He denies any palpitations or irregular heartbeat.
- Pulm: The patient reports feeling short of breath. He denies any cough, wheezing, or sputum production.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms such as frequency, urgency, or pain.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports the chest pain radiating to his left arm. He denies any joint pain or muscle pain.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any headaches, dizziness, numbness, tingling, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any symptoms related to diabetes or thyroid dysfunction.
- Skin: The patient denies any skin rashes, lesions, or changes in color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He reports being non-compliant with his medications.
Medications: The patient is not currently taking any medications.
PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.
FamHx: The patient's father had a myocardial infarction at the age of 55. His mother is alive with no significant medical history.
SocHx: The patient is married and works as an office manager. He denies any tobacco or alcohol use. He exercises irregularly and admits to a poor diet.

Given the patient's presentation of chest pain, radiating to the left arm, and associated shortness of breath, there is concern for a possible acute coronary syndrome. EKG and cardiac enzyme testing will be done to evaluate for possible myocardial infarction. The patient will be placed on a cardiac monitor, vital signs will be monitored, and supplemental oxygen will be provided as needed. An IV line will be established to administer medications as necessary. The cardiology service will be consulted for further management.
