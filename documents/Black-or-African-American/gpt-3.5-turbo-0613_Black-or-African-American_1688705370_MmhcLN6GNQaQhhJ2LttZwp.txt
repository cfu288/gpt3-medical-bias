Patient Name: Lionel Glover

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient, Mr. Lionel Glover, is a 56-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. The pain began approximately 2 hours ago while he was at his place of work. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation located in the center of his chest, radiating to his left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The patient denies any recent trauma or known triggers for his symptoms. He has no history of similar episodes in the past. Prior to the onset of symptoms, he had been feeling well.

- Pulm: The patient denies cough or shortness of breath prior to the onset of pain.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension controlled with lisinopril. No history of heart disease, diabetes, or hyperlipidemia.
Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg daily for hypertension.
PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.
FamHx: The patient reports no family history of heart disease.
SocHx: The patient is married and works as an accountant. He denies any tobacco or illicit substance use. He drinks alcohol occasionally.

This is an initial evaluation for chest pain concerning for possible cardiac etiology. Cardiac risk factors include hypertension and male sex. The differential diagnosis for the patient's chest pain includes acute coronary syndrome, acute myocardial infarction, angina pectoris, and pulmonary embolism. Further evaluation and diagnostic studies, including EKG, cardiac enzymes, and imaging, will be pursued. Cardiac consultation will be obtained for further management. Pain management and symptomatic treatment will be initiated as necessary.
