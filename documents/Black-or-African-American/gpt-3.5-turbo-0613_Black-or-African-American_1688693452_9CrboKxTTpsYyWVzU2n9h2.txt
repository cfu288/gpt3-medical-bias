Patient Name: Cleo Johnson
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Cleo Johnson, is a 52-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain began approximately 2 hours ago while she was at home watching television. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, nausea, or diaphoresis. She has never experienced this type of pain before. The patient reports that the pain is persistent and has not subsided since its onset.

ROS:
- General: No fever, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: No headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: No palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: No cough or shortness of breath.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No focal weakness, numbness, or tingling.
- Endocrine: No excessive thirst or urination.
- Skin: No rash or skin lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. She takes lisinopril for hypertension and atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg daily, atorvastatin 40 mg daily.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a myocardial infarction at the age of 60. Her mother is alive and has a history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes. There is no family history of early cardiac diseases or sudden cardiac death.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She works as an office manager and denies any significant stressors in her life.

Based on the patient's symptoms of chest pain radiating to the left arm, along with her risk factors for cardiovascular disease, concern for acute coronary syndrome is high. Further evaluation with an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray will be performed.
