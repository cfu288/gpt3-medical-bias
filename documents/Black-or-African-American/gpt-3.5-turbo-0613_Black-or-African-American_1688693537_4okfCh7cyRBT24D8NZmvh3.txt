Patient Name: Linwood Dawson 
Age: 57

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: Mr. Dawson is a 57-year-old male presenting to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started suddenly approximately 4 hours ago and is described as a squeezing sensation in the center of the chest. The pain radiates to the left arm and jaw. The patient rates the intensity of the pain as 8 out of 10. The patient denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. Upon further questioning, the patient reveals that he has a history of smoking for 30 years and currently smokes about a pack of cigarettes per day. He has not sought medical attention for his chest pain until now.

- Skin: The patient denies any skin rashes, lesions, or changes in texture.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history significant for tobacco use disorder.

PSurgHx: The patient has no previous surgical history.

FamHx: The patient's father had a myocardial infarction at the age of 65. No other significant family history of heart disease.

SocHx: The patient is currently employed as a construction worker. He is divorced and lives alone. He has a history of smoking for 30 years and drinks alcohol occasionally. He denies any illicit drug use.
