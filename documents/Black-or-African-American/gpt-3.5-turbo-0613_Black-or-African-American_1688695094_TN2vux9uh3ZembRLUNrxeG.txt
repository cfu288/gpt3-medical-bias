Patient Name: Claudette Johnson 
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 52-year-old female presenting to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain is located in the central chest and described as a sharp, squeezing sensation. It started approximately 2 hours ago while the patient was at rest. The pain is intermittent and radiates to the left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. There is no alleviating or aggravating factor identified. The patient denies any previous episodes of similar chest pain.

ROS:

- Head: The patient denies any headache or vision changes.
- Eyes: The patient denies any double vision or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports palpitations and occasional lightheadedness.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, sputum production, or wheezing.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or change in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any dysuria or hematuria.

- Neurologic: The patient denies any weakness, numbness, or tingling.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any history of diabetes or thyroid disorder.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes or skin lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity.
Medications: The patient takes metoprolol for hypertension and atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia.
PSurgHx: The patient has no previous surgeries.
FamHx: The patient's father had a history of myocardial infarction at the age of 65.
SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She is married and works as a teacher.

Based on the patient's history and presentation, the differential diagnosis includes acute coronary syndrome, angina, musculoskeletal chest pain, and gastroesophageal reflux disease. Further evaluation will include an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray. The patient will be placed on continuous cardiac monitoring and given sublingual nitroglycerin for possible angina. The cardiology team will be consulted for further evaluation and management.
