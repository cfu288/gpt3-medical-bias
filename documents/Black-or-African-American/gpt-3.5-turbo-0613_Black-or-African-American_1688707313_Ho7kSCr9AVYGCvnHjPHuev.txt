CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Ms. Elysee is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a burning sensation that radiates to her left arm and jaw. She has no history of chest pain before. She reports feeling anxious and fearful since the onset of the pain.

- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.

Medications: Amlodipine 10 mg daily, atorvastatin 40 mg daily

Based on the patient's presentation of acute chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, concerning for cardiac etiology, and the risk factors of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, she will be further evaluated for possible acute coronary syndrome and admitted for further management.
