Patient Name: Demetrius Johnson 
Age: 40

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Johnson is a 40-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a complaint of chest pain. He reports that the pain began approximately two hours ago while he was at home watching television. He describes the pain as a tightness and pressure in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. Mr. Johnson states that the pain is constant and has not changed in intensity since it started. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or lightheadedness. Additionally, he denies any previous episodes of similar chest pain. Mr. Johnson reports that he has a family history of heart disease, with his father having a myocardial infarction at the age of 55. He denies any recent illness or trauma.
