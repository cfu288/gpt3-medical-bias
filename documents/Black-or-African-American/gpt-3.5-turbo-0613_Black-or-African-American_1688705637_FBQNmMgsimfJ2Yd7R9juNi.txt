Patient Name: Jefferson Williamson
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Williamson, is a 45-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of his chest that started suddenly approximately 2 hours ago. He states that the pain is radiating to his left arm and jaw. He rates the pain as 8 out of 10 in intensity and states that it is not relieved with rest. He denies any associated shortness of breath, nausea, diaphoresis, or palpitations. In the past, he has experienced occasional episodes of chest discomfort, but nothing as severe as this.

- Head: No headaches, head trauma, or dizziness reported.
- Eyes: No visual changes, eye pain, or redness reported.
- Cardiovascular: Apart from the chest pain, no recent palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: No shortness of breath, cough, or wheezing.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or change in bowel habits.
- GU: No urinary complaints such as urgency, frequency, or dysuria.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain, stiffness, or swelling.
- Neurologic: No history of seizures, weakness, or numbness.
- Endocrine: No symptoms of polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes, itching, or changes in moles.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD).
Medications: He takes lisinopril 20 mg daily for hypertension and omeprazole 40 mg daily for GERD.
PSurgHx: No previous surgeries reported.
FamHx: The patient's father had a myocardial infarction at the age of 55.
SocHx: The patient is married, has two children, and works as an accountant. He denies smoking, alcohol, or recreational drug use. He exercises regularly and follows a healthy diet.

Note: Chest pain in a middle-aged patient with risk factors for coronary artery disease warrants a thorough evaluation to rule out acute coronary syndrome. The patient will be further evaluated with an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and additional diagnostic studies as deemed necessary. Prompt cardiology consultation is requested for further management and determination of appropriate treatment.
