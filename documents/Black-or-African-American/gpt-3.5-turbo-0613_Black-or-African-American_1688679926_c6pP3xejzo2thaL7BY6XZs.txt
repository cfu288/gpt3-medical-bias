Patient Name: Ernest McDonald
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: Mr. McDonald is a 55-year-old male who presents to the emergency department complaining of chest pain. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates to his left arm. The pain started approximately 1 hour ago while he was mowing his lawn. It is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. He denies any nausea or vomiting. He is currently sitting upright and appears anxious.

ROS:
- General: Patient complains of fatigue and weakness.
- Head: Negative for headache or trauma.
- Eyes: Negative for vision changes.
- Cardiovascular: Positive for chest pain. Negative for palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: Positive for shortness of breath.
- GI: Negative for abdominal pain or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: Negative for urinary frequency, urgency, or pain.
- Musculoskeletal: Negative for joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: Negative for headache, dizziness, or numbness/tingling.
- Endocrine: Negative for polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: Negative for rash or changes in pigmentation.

PMHx: Hypertension for the past 10 years, on Lisinopril. Hyperlipidemia for the past 5 years, on Atorvastatin. Type 2 diabetes for the past 15 years, on metformin. Denies any history of coronary artery disease, myocardial infarction, or heart failure.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg daily, Atorvastatin 40 mg daily, and Metformin 1000 mg twice daily.

PSurgHx: Appendectomy 30 years ago.

FamHx: Father had a history of myocardial infarction at age 65. Mother has history of hypertension. No siblings.

SocHx: Denies tobacco use. Occasional alcohol use. Sedentary lifestyle. Works as a sedentary office worker.

Review of EKG shows elevated ST segments in leads II, III, and aVF, indicating possible inferior myocardial infarction. Given the patient's history of chest pain, associated symptoms, and EKG findings, acute coronary syndrome is suspected. Emergency lab work including cardiac enzymes and a chest X-ray has been ordered. The patient is currently receiving oxygen therapy and an IV has been established for administration of medications as needed.
