Patient Name: Jacklyn Lynch

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient, Ms. Lynch, is a 45-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She reports that the pain started suddenly approximately two hours ago during exercise. The pain is described as a sharp, stabbing sensation that worsens with deep breaths and movement. The pain radiates to her left shoulder and arm. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, dizziness, or palpitations. Ms. Lynch has no prior history of chest pain. She denies any recent illnesses or injuries. She reports that she had a similar episode of chest pain several years ago, which was diagnosed as musculoskeletal in nature. She states that this pain feels similar but more severe. She denies any recent travel, surgery, or significant changes in her daily routine.

Medications: Amlodipine 5 mg once daily, simvastatin 20 mg once daily.

Based on the patient's symptoms, history, and risk factors, a comprehensive evaluation will be performed to rule out cardiac causes for her chest pain.
