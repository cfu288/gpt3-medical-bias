Patient Name: Wilbert Harris 
Age: 56

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Harris, is a 56-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. He reports that the pain started approximately two hours prior to arrival and is located in the center of his chest. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation that radiates to his left arm. He rates the pain as 8/10 in intensity. The pain is constant and is not relieved with rest or change in position. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. There is no history of trauma or recent illness.

- Head: The patient denies headaches, dizziness, or loss of consciousness.

- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, sputum production, or shortness of breath.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, diarrhea, constipation, or vomiting.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urine color or frequency.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pains, swelling, or limited range of motion.

- Endocrine: The patient denies any excessive thirst, frequent urination, or changes in appetite.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, itching, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He is also known to be a former smoker but has quit approximately 10 years ago.

Medications: The patient takes lisinopril 20mg daily for his hypertension and atorvastatin 40mg daily for his hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: The patient has no history of any surgical procedures.

FamHx: The patient's father had a history of myocardial infarction at the age of 60. His mother is alive with no significant medical history.

Based on the patient's history and presentation of chest pain radiating to the left arm, further evaluation, including an EKG and cardiac enzymes, will be conducted to rule out acute coronary syndrome.
