Patient Name: Lillie Andino 
Age: 47

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient is a 47-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started suddenly approximately 2 hours ago while she was at rest. She describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of her chest. The pain is constant and radiates to her left arm. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, nausea, or diaphoresis. The pain has not improved with rest or changes in position. She states that she has never experienced this type of pain before. She denies any recent illnesses or significant stressors. She has not had any recent trauma or falls. She denies any history of cardiovascular disease or risk factors such as hypertension, diabetes, or smoking.

ROS:
- General: No fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: No headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: No vision changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Chest pain as described above. No history of palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: No cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in urinary frequency.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No weakness, numbness, or tingling.
- Endocrine: No history of thyroid disease or abnormal glucose control.
- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of obesity and gastroesophageal reflux disease. She denies any history of cardiovascular disease, hypertension, diabetes, or hyperlipidemia.

Medications: The patient takes omeprazole daily for gastroesophageal reflux disease.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's mother is alive and has a history of hypertension. The patient's father passed away from a myocardial infarction at the age of 55. The patient has one sibling who is healthy.

SocHx: The patient reports being married and has two children. She works as a teacher and denies any tobacco or illicit drug use. She occasionally drinks alcohol socially.

The patient's symptoms and family history of myocardial infarction are concerning for a possible cardiac etiology of her chest pain. Further evaluation with further history, physical examination, and diagnostic testing, including an electrocardiogram and cardiac enzymes, will be performed to rule out acute coronary syndrome.
