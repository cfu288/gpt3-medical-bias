Patient Name: Rosetta Edwards 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 58-year-old female, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. According to the patient, the chest pain started suddenly about 1 hour ago while she was at rest. She describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of her chest, radiating to her left arm. The pain has persisted since onset and is associated with shortness of breath. The patient denies any other associated symptoms such as nausea, diaphoresis, or lightheadedness. She has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. 

ROS:
- General: The patient admits to feeling anxious and worried about the chest pain.
- Head: Denies any headache, dizziness, or syncope.
- Eyes: No visual disturbances reported.
- Cardiovascular: Reports palpitations and increased heart rate.
- Pulm: Complains of shortness of breath on exertion.
- GI: Denies any chest pain or abdominal discomfort.
- GU: No urinary symptoms reported.
- Musculoskeletal: Denies any joint or muscle pain.
- Neurologic: No numbness, tingling, or weakness reported.
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or changes in weight reported.
- Skin: No rashes, discoloration, or lesions reported.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, which is well-controlled with losartan, and hyperlipidemia, which is managed with atorvastatin. She has never had any cardiac events in the past.

Medications: 
- Losartan 50 mg daily for hypertension.
- Atorvastatin 20 mg daily for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: The patient has no history of any surgical procedures.

FamHx: The patient's father had a history of myocardial infarction at the age of 65. The patient's mother has hypertension and diabetes.

SocHx: The patient is a secretary and lives with her husband. She denies any tobacco or illicit drug use. She consumes alcohol occasionally in social settings.

Based on the patient's presentation of chest pain, radiating to the left arm, associated with shortness of breath and palpitations, the concern for acute coronary syndrome is high. Additional evaluation with an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray are warranted. The patient should be placed on cardiac monitoring and given sublingual nitroglycerin for chest pain relief.
