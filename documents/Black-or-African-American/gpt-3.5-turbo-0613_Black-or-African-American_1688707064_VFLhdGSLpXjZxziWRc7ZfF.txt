Patient Name: Jamila Shuford 
Age: 47

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mrs. Jamila Shuford is a 47-year-old female who presents to the emergency department complaining of severe chest pain that started suddenly about two hours ago. She describes the pain as a crushing sensation that radiates to her left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any nausea or vomiting. The patient states that this is the first time she has experienced chest pain of this severity. She denies any recent trauma or injury and reports no previous history of cardiac-related conditions. She has not taken any medications prior to the onset of the pain.

- Cardiovascular: Aside from the chest pain, the patient denies any palpitations or irregular heartbeats.
- Pulm: The patient reports shortness of breath associated with the chest pain.

- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

Medications: The patient is not currently taking any medications.
PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.
FamHx: There is no known family history of cardiac disease, stroke, or other significant medical conditions.
