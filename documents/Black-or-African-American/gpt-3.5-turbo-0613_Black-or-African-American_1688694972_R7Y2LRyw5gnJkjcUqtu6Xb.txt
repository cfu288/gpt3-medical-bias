Patient Name: Franklyn Skaggs
Age: 56

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Skaggs, is a 56-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaint of chest pain. He reports that the pain started approximately 2 hours ago, is substernal, and radiates to his left arm. He rates the pain as 8/10 in intensity and describes it as squeezing and pressure-like in nature. The pain is associated with diaphoresis, shortness of breath, and nausea. He denies any exacerbating or relieving factors. He states that he has never experienced this type of chest pain before and is concerned that he may be having a heart attack.

- Head: Denies headache or dizziness
- Eyes: Denies any visual changes or double vision
- Cardiovascular: Reports chest pain as described above. Denies palpitations or irregular heartbeat.
- Pulm: Reports shortness of breath associated with chest pain. Denies cough or wheezing.
- GI: Denies any abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting.
- GU: Denies any genitourinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: Denies any joint pain or muscle weakness.
- Neurologic: Denies any weakness or tingling in the extremities.
- Endocrine: No complaints of heat or cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes, itching, or skin changes.

PMHx: Hypertension, hyperlipidemia. No history of coronary artery disease, myocardial infarction, or significant cardiac events.

Medications: Lisinopril 10mg daily, atorvastatin 20mg daily.

PSurgHx: No history of any surgical procedures.

FamHx: Father with hypertension and mother with diabetes. No history of any cardiac events in immediate family.

SocHx: Non-smoker, denies alcohol or illicit drug use. Works as an accountant, sedentary lifestyle.

Based on the patient's presentation of chest pain radiating to the left arm, associated with diaphoresis, shortness of breath, and nausea, there is concern for an acute coronary syndrome. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, placing him at increased risk for cardiac events. Further evaluation is warranted, including an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest X-ray, to assess for possible myocardial infarction or other cardiac pathology.
