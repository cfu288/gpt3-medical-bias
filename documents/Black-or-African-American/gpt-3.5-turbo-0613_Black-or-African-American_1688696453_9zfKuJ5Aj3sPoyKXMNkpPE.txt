Patient Name: Edward Hanible 
Age: 60

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 60-year-old male, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. He reports that the pain started approximately 2 hours ago and has been constant since then. The pain is located in the center of his chest and radiates to his left arm. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation and rates it 8/10 in severity. He denies any shortness of breath, dizziness, or diaphoresis. The patient states that he has never experienced this type of pain before and it is concerning to him.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, or fatigue.
- Head: The patient denies any headache or head trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies any changes in vision or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient complains of chest pain as mentioned in the HPI. He denies any palpitations or prior history of heart disease.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports pain radiating to his left arm but denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any weakness, numbness, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any symptoms of diabetes or thyroid disease.
- Skin: The patient denies any rash or changes in skin integrity.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He reports being compliant with his medications and regular follow-up visits with his primary care physician.

Medications: Lisinopril 20 mg daily, Atorvastatin 40 mg daily, Metformin 1000 mg twice daily

PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgeries.

FamHx: There is a family history of coronary artery disease in both of the patient's parents. His father had a heart attack at age 55 and his mother underwent a coronary artery bypass graft in her 60s.

SocHx: The patient is married and is retired. He denies tobacco or alcohol use.

Based on the patient's presenting symptoms, medical history, and family history, the possibility of myocardial infarction or angina should be considered. Further testing and evaluation are warranted to determine the cause of the patient's chest pain.
