Patient Name: Ulysses Diouf 
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Diouf is a 55-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain began suddenly approximately 2 hours ago while he was at rest. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in his chest that radiates to his left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. He denies any nausea or vomiting. He has no medical history of cardiac disease or previous episodes of chest pain. He denies any recent trauma or illness. He denies any exertion or emotional stress prior to the onset of the pain. He states that the pain is progressively worsening and has not been relieved by rest or over-the-counter pain medication.

- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or double vision.

- Pulm: The patient denies any cough or shortness of breath at rest other than the current symptom of chest pain.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, or weakness in any body part.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any history of diabetes or thyroid disease.
- Skin: The patient denies any rash or skin changes.

PMHx: The patient has no known medical history and is not currently under the care of any physicians.

Medications: The patient does not take any medications regularly and denies any current use of medications.

PSurgHx: The patient has no history of any surgical procedures.

FamHx: The patient has a family history of hypertension in his father and a history of stroke in his mother. There is no family history of cardiac disease or sudden death at a young age.

Based on the patient's presentation and risk factors for cardiovascular disease, the possibility of acute coronary syndrome, such as a myocardial infarction, must be thoroughly evaluated.
