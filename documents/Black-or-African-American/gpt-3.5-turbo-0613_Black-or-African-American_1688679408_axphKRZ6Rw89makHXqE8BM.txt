Patient Name: Josephine Mitchell 
Age: 55

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Ms. Mitchell is a 55-year-old female presenting to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in the center of her chest, radiating to her left arm. She states that the pain started approximately 2 hours ago and has been constant since then. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, palpitations, or diaphoresis. She states that she has never experienced chest pain like this before. The pain is not relieved with rest or over-the-counter pain medications.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: The patient denies any headache, trauma, or dizziness.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports the chest pain as described above. No history of previous cardiac issues or heart disease.
- Pulm: The patient denies cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: The patient denies any nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain or muscle weakness.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, or changes in coordination.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any known endocrine disorders or changes in appetite or thirst.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, itching, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient reports a history of hypertension controlled with medications. No known history of diabetes, heart disease, or other chronic illnesses.

Medications: Amlodipine 5 mg daily for hypertension.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension in both parents. No known family history of heart disease or other significant medical conditions.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies any alcohol or recreational drug use. She lives alone and is independent with activities of daily living.

Based on the patient's history of chest pain radiating to the left arm and the description of the pain as a squeezing sensation, a potential cardiovascular etiology such as angina or myocardial infarction should be considered. Further evaluation and investigations are warranted to rule out any acute cardiac event.
