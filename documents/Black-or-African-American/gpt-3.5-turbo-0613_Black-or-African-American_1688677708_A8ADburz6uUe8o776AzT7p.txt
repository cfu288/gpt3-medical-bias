Patient Name: Patience Matthews 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient is a 58-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a constant, dull ache in her chest, radiating down her left arm. The pain has been present for the past 2 hours and is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. There is no alleviating or exacerbating factors mentioned. The patient denies any recent trauma or physical exertion prior to the onset of symptoms. She states that her past medical history is unremarkable and denies any previous episodes of chest pain. She does mention a family history of coronary artery disease, with both her father and brother having heart attacks in their early 60s.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, or weight loss. She reports feeling fatigued and lightheaded.
- Head: No headache or head injury reported.
- Eyes: No visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports chest pain and shortness of breath. She denies palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: The patient denies cough, wheezing, or sputum production.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain reported.
- GU: No urinary complaints or changes in urinary frequency.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports pain radiating down her left arm. She denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, weakness, or changes in sensation.
- Endocrine: No symptoms of polyuria, polydipsia, or temperature intolerance reported.
- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or changes in pigmentation.

PMHx: The patient reports no significant past medical history. She denies any chronic medical conditions or prior surgeries.

Medications: The patient does not take any regular medications. She denies any known allergies to medications.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgeries.

FamHx: There is a significant family history of coronary artery disease, with both the patient's father and brother having heart attacks in their early 60s.

SocHx: The patient is married and lives with her husband. She is a retired teacher and denies any smoking, alcohol, or illicit drug use. She exercises regularly and follows a healthy diet.

With the above history in mind, the patient's chief complaint of chest pain, associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis, is concerning for a possible cardiac ischemic event. Further investigation is warranted, including an ECG, cardiac enzymes, and close monitoring for any changes in her clinical status.
