Patient Name: Gloria Walker
Age: 62

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Ms. Walker is a 62-year-old female presenting to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a constant, pressure-like sensation located in the center of her chest. The pain has been present for the past 2 hours and is not relieved by rest. She denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea. She has no history of similar symptoms in the past.

- Head: No headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: No blurry vision or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: No palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: No cough or shortness of breath.

- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or stiffness.
- Neurologic: No weakness or numbness.
- Endocrine: No polyuria or polydipsia.
- Skin: No rashes or lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia.
Medications:
- Lisinopril 10mg daily for hypertension.
- Atorvastatin 40mg daily for hyperlipidemia.

PSurgHx: No significant surgical history.
FamHx: The patient's mother had a history of hypertension and is deceased. Her father has a history of coronary artery disease.
SocHx: The patient is a retired teacher, lives alone, denies tobacco use, and drinks alcohol occasionally. She does not have any occupational exposures.

Based on the patient's medical history, presentation, and risk factors, the possibility of cardiac etiology for her chest pain is of concern. A comprehensive evaluation will be performed including an EKG and cardiac enzymes to evaluate for acute coronary syndrome. Pain relief will also be initiated according to protocol.
