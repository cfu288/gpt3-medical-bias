Patient Name: Shelton Pressley
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Pressley is a 58-year-old gentleman who presented to the emergency department with chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started approximately 30 minutes prior to arrival, while he was resting at home. He describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation that radiates to his left arm. He rates the intensity of the pain as a 7 out of 10. The pain is not relieved with rest nor exacerbated by exertion. He denies any shortness of breath, dizziness, or lightheadedness. He denies any palpitations or syncope. He also denies any associated symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or diaphoresis. He denies any history of similar episodes in the past.

ROS:

- Head: The patient denies any headaches or head injuries.
- Eyes: The patient denies any changes in vision or double vision.
- Cardiovascular: In addition to the chest pain, the patient denies any palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms such as dysuria or hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, or weakness.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any symptoms suggestive of diabetes or thyroid dysfunction.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes or skin lesions.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He reports being compliant with his prescribed medications.
 
Medications:
- Losartan 50 mg daily for hypertension
- Simvastatin 20 mg daily for hyperlipidemia

PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports a family history of hypertension in his mother and father. He denies any family history of cardiac disease or sudden cardiac death.

Given the patient's age, risk factors, and presentation with chest pain radiating to the left arm, further evaluation for potential acute coronary syndrome is warranted.
