Patient Name: Linwood Sistrunk 
Age: 47

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Sistrunk is a 47-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a main complaint of chest pain. He describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation in the center of his chest, which started abruptly while he was resting at home. The pain is radiating to his left arm and is associated with sweating and shortness of breath. He reports no previous similar episodes and denies any recent trauma or drug use. The pain has persisted for the past 30 minutes and has not improved with rest. He also complains of some lightheadedness and nausea. 

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: No headache or head injury reported.
- Eyes: No visual disturbances or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports chest tightness and left arm pain.
- Pulm: Denies cough, shortness of breath (except when associated with chest pain), or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea or vomiting (except when related to chest pain).
- GU: No urinary symptoms.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain or muscle weakness.
- Neurologic: The patient reports lightheadedness.
- Endocrine: No known endocrine disorders.
- Skin: No rashes, lesions, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. There is no known history of heart disease, diabetes, or previous myocardial infarction.

Medications: The patient takes lisinopril 20 mg daily for hypertension and atorvastatin 40 mg daily for hyperlipidemia. He reports being compliant with his medications.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a previous myocardial infarction at the age of 55. No other significant family history.

SocHx: The patient works as an accountant. He is married and lives with his wife and two children. He denies smoking, alcohol consumption, or illicit drug use.

Based on the patient's presentation and risk factors, a cardiac etiology for his chest pain is of concern. The possibility of acute coronary syndrome, including myocardial infarction, will be investigated. The patient will undergo further evaluation, including laboratory tests, EKG, and a cardiac consultation. Pain management and supportive measures will be provided as needed.
