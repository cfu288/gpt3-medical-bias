Patient Name: Derick Lyons 
Age: 48

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Lyons is a 48-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. The pain started approximately 1 hour ago and is described as a squeezing sensation located in the center of his chest. He rates the pain as 8/10 in intensity and states that it radiates to his left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and sweating. He denies any exacerbating or relieving factors. He has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia but is noncompliant with his medications. He admits to a 20-pack-year smoking history but currently denies any tobacco or drug use.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies fevers, chills, fatigue, or weight loss.
- Head: The patient denies headache, syncope, dizziness, or trauma.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports chest pain and shortness of breath. No palpitations or leg swelling.
- Pulm: The patient denies cough, shortness of breath at rest, or wheezing.
- GI: No abdominal pain, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No dysuria, frequency, or hematuria.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports pain radiating down his left arm. No joint pain or stiffness.
- Neurologic: No history of stroke, seizure, or numbness/tingling.
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rash, itching, or skin discoloration.

PMHx: 
- Hypertension
- Hyperlipidemia

Medications: 
- None (noncompliant with medications)

PSurgHx: 
- No significant surgical history

FamHx:
- No significant family history of cardiovascular diseases or hereditary conditions.

SocHx:
- The patient is married and works as a construction worker. He denies any recent travel or exposure to sick contacts.

The patient's presentation is concerning for an acute coronary syndrome given the acute onset of severe chest pain associated with radiation to the left arm, shortness of breath, and diaphoresis. Given the patient's risk factors including a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and smoking, urgent evaluation of cardiac etiology is indicated. Initial management includes obtaining an EKG, troponin levels, and providing oxygen via nasal cannula. Cardiology consultation will be obtained for further evaluation and management.
