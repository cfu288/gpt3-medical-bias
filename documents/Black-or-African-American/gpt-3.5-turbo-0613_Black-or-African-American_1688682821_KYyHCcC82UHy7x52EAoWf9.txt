Patient Name: Camelia Coney
Age: 52

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 52-year-old female, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a substernal squeezing sensation that started suddenly while she was at rest. The pain radiates to her left arm and jaw. She rates the pain as 9/10 in intensity. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The patient denies any recent illness, trauma, or similar episodes in the past. She states that she has never experienced chest pain like this before.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies fever, weight loss, or fatigue.
- Head: The patient denies headache or changes in vision.
- Eyes: The patient denies any eye pain or redness.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports intermittent palpitations but denies any history of heart disease or high blood pressure.
- Pulm: The patient denies cough, shortness of breath, or wheezing.
- GI: The patient denies nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain, swelling, or stiffness reported.
- Neurologic: No history of headaches, dizziness, or seizures.
- Endocrine: No history of diabetes or thyroid disorders.
- Skin: No skin changes or lesions reported.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history significant for high cholesterol and a family history of coronary artery disease. There are no other significant medical conditions reported.

Medications: The patient takes atorvastatin 20 mg daily for high cholesterol.

PSurgHx: The patient has no history of surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a myocardial infarction at the age of 55. Her mother has hypertension and diabetes.

SocHx: The patient is a non-smoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She works as a teacher.

Based on the patient's description of sudden-onset substernal chest pain radiating to the left arm and jaw, associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis, the concern for an acute coronary syndrome must be considered. Urgent evaluation and appropriate diagnostic testing, including an electrocardiogram and cardiac enzymes, are necessary to evaluate for a possible myocardial infarction.
