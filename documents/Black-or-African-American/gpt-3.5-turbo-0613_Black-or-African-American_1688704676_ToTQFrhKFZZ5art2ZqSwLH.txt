Patient Name: Erika Blake 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 45-year-old female named Erika Blake, presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. She describes the pain as a severe, crushing sensation that radiates to her left arm and jaw. The pain began approximately two hours ago while she was at rest. She states that she has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned that she may be having a heart attack. The pain is not relieved with rest. She denies any associated shortness of breath, diaphoresis, or nausea.

- Pulm: No cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits.

- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat/cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes, itching, or changes in skin color.

PMHx: Patient reports a history of hypertension that was diagnosed ten years ago. She states that she takes hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily for blood pressure control. She is otherwise healthy and denies any other significant medical conditions.

Medications: Hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily.

PSurgHx: The patient has not had any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a history of coronary artery disease. Her mother is alive and healthy. She has one sibling who is healthy.

SocHx: The patient is a nonsmoker and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use. She works as a teacher and lives with her husband and two children. She denies any specific stressors or recent changes in her life. She has a healthy lifestyle, exercises regularly, and follows a balanced diet.

Based on the patient's symptoms and risk factors, a thorough evaluation will be conducted to rule out acute coronary syndrome as the cause of her chest pain. Differential diagnoses to consider include myocardial infarction, angina, or musculoskeletal chest pain. Cardiac enzymes, electrocardiogram, and chest x-ray will be ordered. Pain management and appropriate disposition will be determined based on the results and clinical course.
