CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, Mr. Donny Clark, is a 55-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of chest pain. He reports that the pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago while he was resting at home. He describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of his chest that radiates down his left arm. He also reports associated shortness of breath and diaphoresis. The pain has been constant and has not eased with any changes in position or activity. He denies any recent illness, trauma, or strenuous physical activity. There is no history of similar episodes in the past.
