Patient Name: Pamella Dobbins 
Age: 58

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mrs. Dobbins is a 58-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with chief complaint of chest pain. She reports that the pain began approximately 2 hours ago while she was at home. She describes the pain as a crushing sensation in the center of her chest that radiates to her left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any nausea, vomiting, or lightheadedness. She states that she has never experienced this type of pain before and it has not improved with rest or over-the-counter antacids.

ROS:
- General: No fever, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: No headache or head injury.
- Eyes: No vision changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: Denies palpitations or dyspnea at rest.
- Pulm: Reports subjective fever and productive cough.
- GI: No abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary frequency, urgency, or dysuria.
- Musculoskeletal: No joint pain, stiffness, or swelling.
- Neurologic: No focal weakness, numbness, or tingling.
- Endocrine: No polyuria, polydipsia, or heat or cold intolerance.
- Skin: No rashes or changes in skin color.

PMHx: The patient has a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. She is on lisinopril 10 mg daily, atorvastatin 40 mg daily, and metformin 1000 mg twice daily.

Medications: Lisinopril 10 mg daily, atorvastatin 40 mg daily, metformin 1000 mg twice daily.

PSurgHx: No previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient reports that her mother had a history of myocardial infarction at the age of 65. Her father has a history of hypertension.

SocHx: The patient is married and works as a secretary. She denies tobacco, alcohol, or illicit drug use. She exercises regularly and follows a healthy diet.

Given the patient's presentation of chest pain radiating to the left arm, associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis, and her risk factors of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes, a possible cardiac etiology such as acute coronary syndrome (ACS) or myocardial infarction (MI) should be considered. Immediate assessment and appropriate management are warranted.
