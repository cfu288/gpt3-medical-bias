Patient Name: Latasha Mark 
Age: 42

CC: Chest Pain

HPI: The patient is a 42-year-old female who presents to the emergency department with complaint of chest pain. The pain started suddenly about 2 hours ago and is located in the center of her chest. She describes the pain as a squeezing sensation that radiates to her left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and sweating. She denies any nausea, vomiting, or palpitations. The patient has no previous history of chest pain and states that this is the first time she is experiencing this type of pain. She denies any recent illnesses or trauma.

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fevers, chills, or weight loss.
- Head: The patient denies any headaches or dizziness.
- Eyes: The patient denies any changes in vision or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient states that she has been experiencing chest pain and shortness of breath. She denies any past history of heart problems or high blood pressure.
- Pulm: The patient denies any cough, wheezing, or shortness of breath at rest.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms or changes in urination.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient denies any joint pain, swelling, or stiffness.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any numbness, tingling, or weakness in her extremities.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any history of diabetes or thyroid problems.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, sores, or changes in her skin.

PMHx: The patient has a history of asthma, for which she uses an inhaler as needed. She also has a history of seasonal allergies. She denies any other significant medical history.

Medications: The patient takes an albuterol inhaler as needed for asthma symptoms. She denies taking any other medications.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any previous surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's mother has a history of high blood pressure and her father has a history of diabetes. She has no siblings.

SocHx: The patient works as a marketing manager and is married with two children. She denies any tobacco or illicit drug use. She drinks alcohol socially on occasion. She denies any recent travel.

The patient's current presentation with sudden onset chest pain associated with shortness of breath and radiation to the left arm is concerning for a cardiac etiology. Given her age and lack of risk factors for cardiac disease, other potential causes such as musculoskeletal or gastroesophageal etiologies should also be considered. Further evaluation and testing including an EKG, cardiac enzymes, and chest X-ray are warranted to determine the cause of her chest pain.
