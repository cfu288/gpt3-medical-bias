Patient Name: Archie Morris 
Age: 54

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: The patient, a 54-year-old male named Archie Morris, presents to the Emergency Department complaining of chest pain. He describes the pain as a squeezing sensation in his chest that radiates to his left arm. He states that the pain started approximately 2 hours ago while he was at home watching television. He reports that the pain is constant and has not changed in intensity since it began. He denies any other associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, lightheadedness, or diaphoresis. He also denies any recent trauma or exertion that may have precipitated the pain. The patient has never experienced this type of pain before and is concerned that it may be related to a heart problem.

- Head: No headache, dizziness, or trauma.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He is currently not on any medications for these conditions.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any prior surgeries.

FamHx: The patient's father had a history of myocardial infarction at the age of 65. His mother is alive with no significant medical history. There is no family history of early-onset heart disease, diabetes, or cancer.

Based on the patient's history and presentation of chest pain radiating to the left arm, further evaluation is warranted to rule out a cardiac etiology. The differential diagnosis includes acute coronary syndrome, myocardial infarction, angina, and musculoskeletal causes. A complete physical examination, including an electrocardiogram, laboratory tests, and further imaging studies, will be performed to determine the cause of the patient's symptoms. Pain control and monitoring of vital signs will be provided while the diagnostic workup is initiated.
