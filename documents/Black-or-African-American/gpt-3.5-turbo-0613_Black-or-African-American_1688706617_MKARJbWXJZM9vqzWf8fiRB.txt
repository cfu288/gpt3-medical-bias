Patient Name: Alexander Moore 
Age: 45

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mr. Moore is a 45-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with complaints of chest pain. He describes the pain as a sharp, stabbing sensation located in the center of his chest. The pain started suddenly approximately two hours ago while he was at rest. He denies any exacerbating or relieving factors and states that the pain is constant in nature. He rates the intensity of the pain as 8 out of 10 and reports that it radiates to his left arm. He denies any associated symptoms such as shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea, or vomiting. He has no history of similar episodes in the past. 

ROS:
- General: The patient denies any fever, chills, weight loss, or malaise.
- Head: He denies any headaches, dizziness, or trauma to the head.
- Eyes: The patient denies any visual changes or eye pain.
- Cardiovascular: The patient reports the chest pain but denies any palpitations or irregular heartbeat.
- Pulm: The patient denies any shortness of breath, cough, or sputum production.
- GI: The patient denies any abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: The patient denies any urinary symptoms such as frequency, urgency, or dysuria.
- Musculoskeletal: The patient reports radiating pain to his left arm but denies any joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: The patient denies any weakness, numbness, or tingling.
- Endocrine: The patient denies any history of diabetes, thyroid disorders, or adrenal disorders.
- Skin: The patient denies any rashes, itching, or changes in the skin.

PMHx: The patient has a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, both diagnosed 5 years ago. He reports being compliant with his medications and denies any recent changes in his medical management.

Medications: The patient is currently taking lisinopril 10 mg daily for his hypertension and atorvastatin 20 mg daily for his hyperlipidemia. He is not currently taking any over-the-counter medications or herbal supplements.

PSurgHx: The patient denies any significant surgical history.

FamHx: There is no significant family history of cardiovascular diseases, including myocardial infarction or sudden cardiac death.

SocHx: The patient is an accountant and denies any tobacco, alcohol, or illicit drug use. He states that he lives alone and has no significant stressors in his life.

Given the acute onset of severe chest pain radiating to the left arm in a patient with risk factors for cardiovascular disease, concern for myocardial infarction is high. Further evaluation with an ECG, cardiac enzymes, and a chest x-ray is indicated. Pain management will include nitroglycerin as needed for relief.
