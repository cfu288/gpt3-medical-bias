Patient Name: Mamie Middlebrook 
Age: 54

CC: Chest Pain
HPI: Mrs. Middlebrook is a 54-year-old female with no significant past medical history who presents to the emergency department with complaints of substernal chest pain that started one hour prior to arrival. She describes the pain as a pressure-like sensation that radiates to her left arm. The pain is associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis. She denies any recent trauma, fever, cough, or other respiratory symptoms. She also denies any recent travel or exposure to sick contacts. 

ROS:
- General: She reports feeling anxious and fearful due to the chest pain.
- Head: No headache or dizziness.
- Eyes: No visual disturbances.
- Cardiovascular: Positive for chest pain and shortness of breath. Negative for palpitations or edema.
- Pulm: Positive for shortness of breath. Negative for cough, wheezing, or hemoptysis.
- GI: No nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or changes in bowel movements.
- GU: No urinary symptoms or changes in urination.
- Musculoskeletal: Negative for joint pain or swelling.
- Neurologic: No weakness, numbness, or tingling.
- Endocrine: No changes in thirst, appetite, or weight.
- Skin: No rashes or lesions.

PMHx: No significant past medical history reported. 
Medications: No regular medications reported.
PSurgHx: No prior surgeries reported.
FamHx: No significant family history of cardiac diseases or early cardiac events.
SocHx: She is a non-smoker and denies alcohol or illicit drug use. She works as a full-time accountant and lives a sedentary lifestyle.

Given the patient's presentation of substernal chest pain with radiation to the left arm, associated with shortness of breath and diaphoresis, the differential diagnosis includes acute coronary syndrome, pulmonary embolism, aortic dissection, and pericarditis. Further evaluation is needed to determine the cause of her symptoms. Initial management includes obtaining an electrocardiogram, cardiac biomarkers, chest x-ray, and administering oxygen therapy. An urgent consultation with cardiology is recommended.
